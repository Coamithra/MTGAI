**Analysis:**


Overgrown Ambush is a straightforward green combat trick at common rarity. It's an instant that pumps a creature you control by +3/+3 and grants trample until end of turn for {3}{G} (4 mana total).

**Templating & Rules**: The oracle text is correctly formatted and uses modern MTG templating. There are no custom mechanics (Salvage, Malfunction, or Overclock) on this card, so reminder text is not applicable.

**Color Pie**: This effect is firmly within green's slice of the pie. Green gets both significant creature pump and trample naturally. At common, green combat tricks with trample are appropriate (see cards like Ranger's Guile, Aspect of the Badger, etc.). The combination of pump + trample is a classic green combat pattern.

**Balance**: At {3}{G} (4 mana), this is appropriately costed for a common. The card is comparable to Titanic Growth ({2}{G}, +4/+4 no evasion) but trades one point of power for trample and instant-speed flexibility. Holding up 4 mana is a significant investment at common, which naturally balances the effect. This is not above-rate for its cost at this rarity. The instant speed makes it useful for combat tricks, but it's not overpowered — it's a legitimate pump spell with a clear limitation.

**Design**: The card has a focused, single purpose: pump your creature and help it win combat by guaranteeing trample damage. There's no kitchen-sink design here. The flavor text ties the mechanical effect to the set's post-apocalyptic megadungeon theme appropriately.

**Mechanics & Interactions**: No keyword negations, no false variability, no mechanic collisions. The effect is straightforward and works exactly as intended.

**Custom Mechanics**: This card uses no custom mechanics, so reminder text is not required.

This is a clean, well-designed common that fills a necessary role in limited (green combat trick with evasion-granting element).
