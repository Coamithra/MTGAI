**Analysis:**


Subsurface Harvest is a well-designed black uncommon enchantment that fits the set's sacrifice-focused themes. The card has two distinct abilities that complement each other without conflicting:

**Templating & Mechanics:**
The oracle text is cleanly templated and uses standard MTG language. "Whenever a creature you control dies, you may pay 1 life. If you do, draw a card" follows the modern pattern for optional triggered abilities with a cost rider. The second ability uses "if three or more creatures died this turn" which is standard conditional phrasing for turn-end triggers.

**Color Pie:**
Both abilities are solidly black. Life payment for card draw is a hallmark of black (Phyrexian Arena, Dark Prophecy, Necrologia). Creature token generation is black-acceptable, particularly when tied to sacrifice/death themes. The 4/4 Horror creature token fits the set's eldritch aesthetic.

**Balance:**
At {3}{B}{B} (CMC 5), this is appropriately costed for uncommon. The first ability trades 1 life for a card draw, which is a classic B rate with precedent across many sets. The second ability requires setup—three creatures dying in one turn is a meaningful threshold that demands either a sacrifice outlet strategy or a board wipe. The 4/4 Horror token is a solid payoff but not game-breaking since it arrives on the end step and costs a full turn's resource commitment. The card fills a midgame role for sacrifice decks without being oppressive. Compare favorably to cards like Zulaport Cutthroat or Cartel Aristocrat strategies at similar power level and mana cost.

**Design Quality:**
The card has a focused purpose: rewarding sacrifice-heavy strategies with card draw and creature tokens. There's no kitchen sink bloat—both abilities feed into the core sacrifice theme. The variability is real: the number of creatures dying can range from 1–5+ in a given turn, making the card draw amount genuinely variable, and the 4/4 token only triggers when the threshold is met, creating meaningful deckbuilding decisions.

**No Custom Mechanics:**
This card contains no Salvage, Malfunction, or Overclock keywords, so reminder text requirements do not apply.

**Keyword Interactions:**
No keywords appear on this card, so there are no negated or redundant keyword interactions.

**Conditionals:**
The "if three or more creatures died this turn" conditional is meaningful—in many game states, fewer than three creatures will die, making the second ability a real reward for sacrifice-focused plays rather than a redundant bonus.
