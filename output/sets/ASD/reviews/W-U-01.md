**Analysis:**

Cult Archivist is a straightforward artifact-synergy payoff in white at uncommon. Let me evaluate it systematically:

**Templating & Mechanics:**
The oracle text is clean and uses standard MTG templating. "Whenever an artifact enters under your control" is the correct modern phrasing for this trigger. No custom mechanics appear on this card, so no reminder text is needed. The card is templating-correct.

**Color Pie:**
Lifegain on artifact entry is solidly in white's color pie. White is the primary lifegain color, and caring about artifacts is color-neutral (though slightly pushed in white and blue in this set). This aligns well with the design notes' claim that it parallels similar effects in red (Spark Detonator) and green (Spore-Nest Forager), giving white its color-appropriate slice.

**Power Level & Balance:**
At {W} for a 1/1, this is a build-around uncommon that rewards playing artifacts. The comparison points are helpful:
- For {W}, compare to cards like Satyr Wayfinder (which is {1}{G}, a 1/1 that searches on enter) or Viscera Seer ({1}{B}, a 1/1 that sacrifices for value)
- The effect is small (1 life per artifact) but can accumulate across a draft deck with multiple artifact entries
- In the artifact subtheme, this acts as a synergy piece rather than a high-impact payoff
- The 1/1 body is inherently fragile, which keeps this from being oppressive
- Uncommon is the right rarity for a synergy piece that rewards deck-building

The card is fair at uncommon. It's not format-breaking, but provides real value in the artifact-focused white deck. The incremental lifegain is a reasonable reward that doesn't dominate the format.
