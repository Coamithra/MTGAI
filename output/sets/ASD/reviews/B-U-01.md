**Analysis:**

Toothwork Familiar is a clean, focused black uncommon 1-drop that fits well into the set's sacrifice/aristocrats themes. The design is straightforward: a 1/1 body for one black mana with a death trigger that drains each opponent for 1 while you gain 1 life.

**Templating & Mechanics:** The oracle text is correctly templated. No custom mechanics are present, so no reminder text is required. The death trigger uses proper modern templating ("When ~ dies").

**Color Pie:** This effect is solidly in black's slice. Life drain/drain on death effects are classic black mechanics, and the card fits the archetype of small creatures with valuable death triggers that reward sacrifice strategies. The flavor of a construct made from teeth is appropriately dark and fits the dungeon theme.

**Balance:** At uncommon, a 1/1 for B that drains 1 on death is appropriately costed. Comparable reference points:
- Zulaport Cutthroat (2-mana 1/1 with a similar drain) is above this rate
- Viscera Seer (2-mana 1/1 with no drain, just sacrifice utility) is comparable
- This card sits naturally at 1 mana because the drain is delayed until death

The power-toughness ratio (P+T=2) is well under budget for the cost, which is appropriate for a utility creature. The card rewards the creature dying, which creates gameplay tension and synergy with sacrifice outlets. It won't dominate limited, but it's a valuable role-player that encourages specific deck building.

**Design Quality:** The card has a single, focused purpose: be a cheap body that provides value when sacrificed or traded. It encourages the player to interact with their own creatures, fitting into the aristocrats archetype. No kitchen sink design here. The flavor perfectly supports the mechanical purpose and the set's theme.
