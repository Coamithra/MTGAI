## Original Card

> Name: The Brain Engine
> Mana Cost: {3}{B}{B}
> Type: Legendary Enchantment -- Artifact
> Rarity: rare
> Oracle Text: Whenever a nontoken creature you control dies, exile it with a charge counter on ~.
> {2}{B}, {T}, Remove three charge counters from ~: Return target creature card from exile to the battlefield under your control. It's a black Zombie in addition to its other colors and types.
> Flavor Text: Feretha's body sat on the throne. His brain hung in the basement, dreaming of a city it no longer controlled.
> Design Notes: A legendary enchantment artifact representing the machine keeping Feretha's brain alive -- and the broader super-science of brain extraction in the setting. It stockpiles dead creatures (exiling them to track via charge counters) then reanimates from exile, not graveyard, which is a distinctive twist. Requiring 3 charge counters means you need three creatures to die before you get one back -- this is a slow engine, not a combo piece. The Zombie typing adds black flavor. The tap requirement means one activation per turn. At 5 mana with a 3-death investment before payoff, this is powerful but appropriately slow for a rare build-around. Raise dead from exile is black's reanimation wheelhouse.

## Council Reviews

### Reviewer 1

**Verdict:** OK (0 issues)  
**Cost:** $0.0049 (2,505 in / 727 out)

The Brain Engine is a well-designed rare that represents a focused mechanical identity: a graveyard-adjacent engine that taxes resources (three creature deaths per activation) while offering meaningful reanimation from exile rather than the graveyard. This is mechanically distinct and flavorfully cohesive.

**Templating & Mechanics:**
The oracle text is correctly templated. The second ability properly specifies "{2}{B}, {T}, Remove three charge counters" as the cost, with the tap requirement ensuring one activation per turn. Exiling creatures with charge counters is a clean way to track stockpiled creatures without relying on graveyard manipulation directly.

**Balance & Power Level:**
At 5 mana on a legendary enchantment-artifact, the power level is appropriate for rare. The cost structure requires significant investment: (1) 5 mana to play, (2) three creature deaths to accumulate charge counters, (3) {2}{B} + tap + three counters per activation. This is slow and deliberate—not a combo piece. The payoff (a reanimated creature that becomes a black Zombie) is good but gated behind meaningful resource investment. Comparable to similar reanimation engines like Lurrus of the Dream-Den (though that's companion) or Silversmote Ghoul, but slower and more demanding. This is appropriately powerful for a rare build-around in a graveyard-adjacent black archetype.

**Design Quality:**
The card has a single, laser-focused purpose: convert dead creatures into exile-based resources, then convert those resources into bodies on the battlefield. No kitchen sink problems. The Zombie typing adds a small flavor/mechanical touch (synergizes with any zombie lords or theme) without being bloat. The exile-based reanimation is distinctive and avoids generic graveyard recursion fatigue.

**Color Pie:**
Solid black reanimation. The legendary status and artifact typing fit the fictional "brain machine" flavor without violating color pie. Exile-based reanimation is becoming increasingly blue/black, and this leans appropriately into black's wheelhouse.

**Custom Mechanics:**
This card does NOT use any custom mechanics from Anomalous Descent (Salvage, Malfunction, or Overclock). The charge counter mechanic is a standard MTG mechanic used on planeswalkers and other cards, so no reminder text needed.

**No Redundancies or Negations:**
No abilities are negated. The card is not a creature, so there are no vanilla stats to make irrelevant. The legendary typing is appropriate and flavorful.

**Charge Counter Tracking:**
The use of charge counters to track exiled creatures is clean and follows MTG conventions. Clearly searchable game state.

This is a strong, flavorful rare that will function well as a black graveyard-matters build-around. No issues detected.
