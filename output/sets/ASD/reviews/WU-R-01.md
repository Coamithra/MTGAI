## Original Card

> Name: Protonium Curator
> Mana Cost: {W}{U}
> Type: Legendary Creature — Human Artificer
> Rarity: rare
> Oracle Text: Whenever an artifact enters under your control, you may tap or untap target artifact.
> Artifacts you control have "Malfunction 1" (This permanent enters tapped with one malfunction counter on it. At the beginning of your upkeep, remove a malfunction counter from it.) instead of "Malfunction 2" or "Malfunction 3."
> Flavor Text: "Every relic tells me its name. Most of them are screaming it."
> P/T: 1/3
> Design Notes: Rare legendary for the WU Ancient Technology archetype. The first ability -- tapping or untapping an artifact when one enters -- is a flexible control tool. It can lock down an opponent's artifact or untap your own for repeated use, creating interesting board puzzles. The second ability is a build-around that reduces Malfunction counters on your permanents, making your degraded automatons come online faster. This directly supports the WU archetype's theme of mastering ancient technology. A 1/3 body is defensive and appropriate for a 2-mana utility legend -- it survives common removal like Spark Saboteur's ping. White gets the tap/untap-artifact space, blue gets artifact affinity, and the Malfunction reduction is a set-specific synergy payoff. The Malfunction reduction is a static replacement effect that's clean to parse.

## Council Reviews

### Reviewer 1

**Verdict:** OK (0 issues)  
**Cost:** $0.0052 (2,969 in / 715 out)


Protonium Curator is a legendary 2-mana WU Artificer that provides two related effects: (1) flexible tap/untap control when artifacts enter, and (2) a Malfunction cost reduction from 2-3 to 1.

**Templating & Custom Mechanics:**
The oracle text correctly uses the custom "Malfunction 1" keyword with proper reminder text on its first appearance. The mechanics are well-templated and clearly referenced. The reminder text accurately describes Malfunction's behavior.

**Keyword Interaction Analysis:**
The first ability (tap/untap target artifact) is genuinely flexible and creates tactical gameplay depth. It enables both disruption (tapping opponent's artifacts) and offense (untapping your own for reuse). This synergizes naturally with the Malfunction reduction theme.

**Balance & Power Level:**
At {W}{U} for a 1/3 legendary, the baseline is reasonable. The first ability is pure value—each artifact trigger grants a tap/untap option, which is useful but not game-breaking. The second ability (Malfunction reduction) is a build-around payoff that's contextual: it only matters in decks specifically building around Malfunction artifacts. In Limited, this would be a strong signpost for the WU archetype but not oppressive. In Constructed, it requires supporting artifact synergies. The 1/3 body is appropriately fragile for a utility legend. This is at-rate or slightly above-rate for a rare, which is acceptable given its archetype-dependent nature.

**Design & Purpose:**
The card has clear focus: it's a reward for playing Malfunction artifacts and a control tool for artifact decks. It's not kitchen sink—both abilities are artifact-focused and synergistic. The build-around nature (reduction only applies to your own permanents) prevents abuse.

**Color Pie:**
White has tap/untap effects for artifacts (e.g., Tocatli Honor Guard, Stoneforge Mystic context), and blue has artifact synergy and cost reduction mechanics. The combination is cohesive for {W}{U} Artificer.

**Issues Found:**
The first ability's phrasing "you may tap or untap target artifact" triggers on each artifact entry, providing repeatable options. This is clean and intentional. The second ability's phrasing as a replacement effect ("instead of Malfunction 2 or Malfunction 3") correctly avoids stacking issues. No ambiguities detected.

One minor note: the design notes mention a "static replacement effect," which is correct terminology for how the Malfunction reduction works, but the oracle text itself is phrased as a standard ability rather than explicitly using replacement language. This is fine—the meaning is clear and follows modern templating.

The card serves its intended purpose as a WU archetype signpost and delivers meaningful gameplay without exploitable interactions or balance issues.
