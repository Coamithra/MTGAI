**Analysis:**

Catacomb Harvester is a clean, well-designed black uncommon that fits squarely within the color pie and set context. The card has two straightforward abilities: deathtouch (a keyword) and a death-triggered card draw engine that costs 1 life.

**Templating & Mechanics:** The oracle text is correctly templated. "Whenever another creature you control dies, you may pay 1 life. If you do, draw a card." is standard conditional sacrifice-reward text. No custom mechanics appear on this card, so no reminder text is needed.

**Power Level & Balance:** A 2/2 for {2}{B} with deathtouch is fair. The P+T sum is 4, which equals CMC+1—well within the budget for a creature with a meaningful keyword. The triggered ability adds value in grindy, sacrifice-heavy strategies. The life payment (1 life per activation) prevents this from being free card draw and creates meaningful tension. Compared to cards like Zulaport Cutthroat (2/1 for {1}{B}, triggers on death but drains for 1 instead of drawing), Catacomb Harvester is appropriately costed for uncommon. The card rewards building around it rather than being a standalone powerhouse.

**Design Quality:** The card has a focused purpose: reward sacrifice strategies in black. It supports the stated archetypes (WB aristocrats with Fist of Denethix, and the mentioned mono-B synergies). The two-ability design (keyword + triggered ability) is appropriate for uncommon complexity. No kitchen sink issues—every part of the card serves the sacrifice/card advantage theme.

**Keyword Interactions:** Deathtouch is a pure upside on a 2/2 body and doesn't conflict with the triggered ability. The death trigger doesn't negate or make deathtouch irrelevant.

**Color Pie:** Black is the color of death, sacrifice, and life payment for value. This card is squarely in black's identity.

**Custom Mechanics:** No Salvage, Malfunction, or Overclock appears on this card, so no custom reminder text is required.

Overall, this is a solid, focused uncommon that belongs in the set.
