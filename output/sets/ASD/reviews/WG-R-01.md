## Original Card

> Name: Sura, Rendon Ranchmaster
> Mana Cost: {1}{W}{G}
> Type: Legendary Creature -- Human Settler
> Rarity: rare
> Oracle Text: Whenever you create one or more tokens, put a +1/+1 counter on each of them.
> {3}{G}{W}, {T}: Create two 1/1 white Human Soldier creature tokens and a 3/3 green Dinosaur creature token.
> Flavor Text: "The beasts don't need taming. They need purpose."
> P/T: 2/3
> Design Notes: Rare legendary for the WG Frontier Settlers archetype. The static ability rewards the entire token strategy by buffing every token you create across all sources -- Human Soldiers from Edict of Continuity arrive as 2/2s, Dinosaur tokens from Reclaim the Surface arrive as 5/5s. The 2/3 body for 3 is modest, keeping the card honest when you don't have token support. Green gets creature tokens and +1/+1 counters; white gets soldier tokens and anthem-adjacent effects. The legendary status prevents doubling up in limited.

### Reviewer 1

Sura, Rendon Ranchmaster is a well-designed rare legendary creature for the WG token/settler archetype. The card has a focused purpose: act as a token payoff/anthem effect that synergizes with the WG tokens strategy while also providing self-contained token generation.

**Templating & Rules**: The oracle text is clean and properly formatted. The static ability is straightforward: whenever tokens are created (by any source), they enter with +1/+1 counters. The activated ability is a mana + tap cost producing three tokens. No custom mechanics appear on this card, so reminder text requirements don't apply.

**Color Pie**: The card is solidly in WG. White handles Soldier tokens and anthem-adjacent effects (buffing tokens). Green handles Dinosaur tokens and +1/+1 counter synergies. The mana cost {1}{W}{G} appropriately represents the three-color identity. The 2/3 body for 3 mana is reasonable for a legendary creature.

**Balance**: At rare, this is a powerful build-around that demands an answer, but it's fairly costed. The 2/3 body for 3 mana is modest when not supported by tokens. The activated ability costs {3}{G}{W} and taps Sura, making it a 5-mana investment to generate 3 tokens (7 power, boosted to 9 power with the +1/+1 counters). The static ability's power scales significantly in token-heavy Limited formats, but that's exactly what makes it a desirable rare—it rewards a specific archetype. The card is strong but not oppressive: it doesn't generate value immediately, lacks evasion, and requires setup turns to activate the ability. Comparison: other token anthems at rare like Doubling Season (which doubles tokens and applies +1/+1 counters) are more expensive mana-wise but more powerful, so Sura seems appropriately positioned.

**Design**: The card has a single, focused mechanic (token anthem) with an optional self-contained token engine. It's not a kitchen sink—both abilities serve the same strategic purpose. The activated ability isn't mandatory, giving players the option to hold Sura back or swing if they prefer. The legendary status prevents limited players from stacking multiple copies, which is correct design.

**Mechanical Issues**: None identified. No negated keywords. No custom mechanics, so no reminder text concerns. No redundant conditionals. No collision with existing MTG keywords.

**Variability**: The static ability's power varies naturally based on how many tokens the player creates (real variability). The activated ability generates fixed tokens, but that's by design.

The card is well-executed and ready for print.

Sura, Rendon Ranchmaster is a well-designed rare legendary creature that serves as a powerful build-around for the WG token archetype. Let me analyze the key aspects:

**Templating & Mechanics:**
The oracle text is correctly templated. The static ability "Whenever you create one or more tokens, put a +1/+1 counter on each of them" uses proper modern templating (current MTG template is "Whenever you create one or more tokens, put a +1/+1 counter on each token created this way," but the card's version is acceptable and conveys the same meaning). The activated ability is clearly templated: {3}{G}{W}, {T}: Create two 1/1 white Human Soldier creature tokens and a 3/3 green Dinosaur creature token. No custom mechanics appear on this card, so no reminder text is required.

**Balance Analysis:**
At 3 mana for a 2/3 body with no immediate impact, the card is relatively modest on rate. However, the static ability is the real payoff. A +1/+1 counter on every token created is a significant boost to the token archetype. The activated ability costs 5 mana total (3 generic + 1 green + 1 white, plus tapping), generating 7 power across 3 bodies (2/2 Soldier + 2/2 Soldier + 4/4 Dinosaur = 8 total power after the counters, or 7 before). This is reasonable for a 5-mana investment on a legendary that needs to survive a turn to use.

Comparing to similar cards: [[Cathars' Crusade]] (3 mana) gives +1/+1 to creatures you control whenever a creature enters. [[Doubling Season]] (5 mana) doubles tokens and counters. [[Ezuri, Renegade Leader]] (2 mana) provides a repeatable token strategy with overhead. Sura at 3 mana for a creature that also taps for tokens is in reasonable territory—not overpowered, but definitely valuable in the right deck.

**Color Pie:**
Green and white are both appropriate colors for creature tokens and +1/+1 counters. The Soldier tokens are white (flavor: Settlers creates soldiers), and the Dinosaur tokens are green. The anthem effect on the static ability is white-aligned (Global pumps are white's specialty). The creature type (Human Settler) and flavor text fit the WG Frontier Settlers archetype perfectly. No color pie violation.

**Design Quality:**
The card has a focused purpose: reward a token-heavy strategy while providing its own token engine. It's not a kitchen sink—it does one thing well. The synergy with the stated archetype is intentional and meaningful. The design notes correctly identify that tokens from other sources (like Edict of Continuity and Reclaim the Surface) are boosted, creating real build-around incentives. The 2/3 body keeps the card "honest" as stated, meaning it's not so efficient that it dominates on stats alone.

**Keyword Issues:**
No keywords are present on this card. No negations or collisions to flag.

**Conditional Logic:**
The static ability has no conditional. The activated ability has no conditional either. Both effects are straightforward.

**Custom Mechanics:**
No Salvage, Malfunction, or Overclock keywords appear on this card, so no reminder text is needed.

**Verdict:**
This card is well-designed, properly templated, appropriately balanced for rare, and cohesive with its intended archetype. No issues to flag.
