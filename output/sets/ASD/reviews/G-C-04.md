**Analysis:**

**Reclaim the Surface** is a green uncommon sorcery at CMC 7 that destroys target artifact and target enchantment, then creates two 4/4 green Dinosaur creature tokens with trample.

**Templating & Mechanics:**
The oracle text is clean and follows modern MTG templating. The card does not use any custom mechanics (Salvage, Malfunction, or Overclock), so no reminder text is required. The wording is straightforward: two modes (destroy artifact, destroy enchantment) as a single effect, followed by a token creation effect. No issues here.

**Balance:**
At CMC 7, this card is being compared to uncommon sorceries with dual-target removal plus token generation. The payoff is:
- Removal of two permanent types (typically 2-3 mana of value if cast separately)
- Two 4/4 tokens with trample (8 power for roughly 4 mana of value)

Total rate is approximately 6-7 mana of value for 7 mana spent—reasonable but not exceptional. The dual-target restriction (artifact AND enchantment) is a significant deckbuilding constraint in limited, making it narrower and conditional. The design notes correctly identify this as acceptable at uncommon because the payoff rewards greedier decks that include both types of targets. This is appropriately balanced: players need to build around it, and whiffing on either target makes the card mediocre. The token generation ensures the card is not a complete dead draw even if you can't hit both targets (you at least get tokens).

Compare to analogues like **Thrashing Brontodon** (2 mana, limited removal) and uncommon sorceries with token generation—this sits in the right power band for uncommon.

**Design Quality:**
The card has a clear, focused purpose: it's a green payoff card for decks that include both artifact and enchantment removal targets, backed by token generation as a fallback. It fits the set's theme (nature reclaiming ancient ruins). The flavor text reinforces the mechanical identity. The design is not a kitchen sink; it's a cohesive green effect.

**Color Pie:**
