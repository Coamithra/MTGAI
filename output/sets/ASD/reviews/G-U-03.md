**Analysis:**

Law of the Wilderness is a thematically strong uncommon finisher that captures green's philosophy of reclaiming the world from artifice. The card has three distinct but coherent abilities:

1. **ETB effect**: Destroy all artifacts and enchantments other than itself. This is appropriately on-color for green (which has access to artifact/enchantment destruction, though traditionally more via creatures) and serves as the "reset button" that clears the board of opposing permanents.

2. **Anthem effect**: +2/+2 and trample to creatures you control. This is classic green payoff and works well with go-wide strategies.

3. **Token generation**: Creates 3/3 Dinosaur tokens whenever an opponent's artifact or enchantment goes to the graveyard. This incentivizes opponents to play around the card and rewards you if they do rebuild.

**Templating & Mechanics**: Oracle text is clean and correctly formatted. The ETB effect uses "other than ~" which is correct modern templating for "other than this permanent." No custom mechanics are present, so no reminder text is needed.

**Balance**: At CMC 7, this is appropriately costed for an uncommon. It's powerful but requires setup and time to come online. Comparable cards:
- Tranquility (3G, WotC) destroys all enchantments for 4 mana
- Shatterstorm (2R, WotC) destroys all artifacts for 3 mana
- This combines both effects + anthem + token generation for 7 total. The power level is reasonable for the cost and conditional nature (requires you to have creatures, requires opponents to have permanents to destroy post-ETB).

The token generation scales based on opponent play patterns, creating interactive decision points. This is appropriate variation, not false variability.

**Design Quality**: The card is focused on a coherent theme: green reclaiming the world from technology. While it has multiple abilities, they all serve this unified purpose rather than being a kitchen sink pile of unrelated effects.

**Color Pie**: All abilities are on-color for green. Green can destroy artifacts and enchantments (especially as an ETB effect, Titania's Song, Reclamation Sage variants). +2/+2 and trample are classic green. Dinosaur token generation is in-color. No issues.

**Interactions**: No keyword negation, no problematic nonbos. The ETB destroying permanents sets up the anthem to be more impactful by clearing opposing blockers.

**Miscellaneous**: No custom mechanics, so no collision issues. No irrelevant "enters tapped" or similar. The card is clean.

Overall, this is a well-designed, thematically unified, and appropriately balanced uncommon finisher.
