**Analysis:**

Fist Checkpoint Warden is a clean, focused white uncommon creature with a straightforward ETB tempo effect. The card presents no issues with MTG templating, mechanic interactions, or color pie adherence.

**Templating:** The oracle text is correctly templated using modern conventions. "It doesn't untap during its controller's next untap step" is the standard way to phrase the lock-down effect, familiar from cards like Kor Hookmaster and Reflector Mage.

**Balance:** At {2}{W} (3 CMC), the 2/3 body is fairly costed (P+T=5, which is on-rate for a white creature at this cost). The ETB effect—tap target creature and prevent its untap—is a meaningful tempo play that scales with the board state but has natural limitations (must have an opponent's creature to target, temporary disruption only). Compared to Reflector Mage (2WU for 2/3 with a similar but conditional bounce effect), this is appropriate for uncommon. The card does not feel underpowered.

**Design Quality:** This is a focused, single-effect design with clear purpose: white's tempo-via-lockdown strategy. The card has one job (tap and freeze) and executes it cleanly. No kitchen sink elements. The flavor tie-in (authoritarian checkpoint) reinforces the mechanical identity without adding unnecessary baggage. The ETB nature makes it a natural fit for blink/flicker synergies noted in the design notes.

**Custom Mechanics:** This card uses no custom mechanics (Salvage, Malfunction, Overclock). No reminder text issues.

**Keyword Interactions:** No negated or useless keywords. The creature has no keywords that conflict with or undermine its ETB effect.

**Color Pie:** Tapping and preventing untap is firmly within white's pie. Blue has done similar effects (Reflector Mage), but white tap-effects are canonical (Humble, Pacifism variants, Kor Hookmaster).

**Conditional Effects:** The "target creature an opponent controls" is a meaningful conditional—if an opponent controls no creatures, the ETB trigger can't be fulfilled. This is real variability with genuine decision points.

**Comparison to Set Mechanics:** The card fits well with the checkpoint/authority flavor of the Unyielding Fist faction and supports the blink/flicker synergies mentioned.

No issues identified. The card is well-designed, properly templated, and appropriately balanced for uncommon.
