**Analysis:**

Edict of Continuity is a white uncommon enchantment that presents two triggered abilities working cohesively within a "civilization endures" thematic framework. Let me evaluate each component:

**Ability 1: Creature Token Generation**
The first ability reads: "Whenever one or more creatures you control die, create a 1/1 white Human Soldier creature token." This is straightforward white design—token generation from creature sacrifice/death is a core white mechanic. The "one or more" templating correctly prevents the token from creating for each individual creature that dies (you don't get 5 tokens if 5 creatures die), which is good design discipline for an uncommon. A 1/1 white token for free recursion is appropriately powered. Comparable: cards like Bitterblossom create tokens on a smaller scale but cost less; this feels appropriately costed for uncommon.

**Ability 2: Artifact Recursion**
The second ability reads: "Whenever an artifact you control is put into a graveyard from the battlefield, you may return it to its owner's hand." This is a white-blue effect (color pie-wise, artifact recursion appears in both colors, though returning to hand is more blue than white). The "put into a graveyard from the battlefield" clause is precise and avoids interaction with discard effects. The optional nature ("you may") is correct—this prevents forced memory issues in edge cases and lets you avoid recursion when disadvantageous. This ability synergizes well with the set's artifact theme and the Salvage mechanic (which encourages running artifacts). However, there's a color pie question worth examining.

**Color Pie Concern**
White in modern Magic can do limited artifact recursion, but returning artifacts directly to hand is primarily a blue effect. White more commonly recurs artifacts to the battlefield (like Emry-style effects, though Emry herself is blue). However, the card is labeled as white, and white has had returning-to-hand effects before (e.g., Restoration Angel effects in limited capacity). For a thematic "civilization endures" card in a custom set, white getting artifact recursion to hand is defensible under the custom set's color pie. The design notes acknowledge this is part of white's "civilization" theme where machines are "repaired" (recovered). I would not flag this as a color pie violation in the context of Anomalous Descent.

**Balance and Templating**
The mana cost is {2}{W}{W} = 4 mana, 2WW. For an uncommon at CMC 4, this is appropriately costed. The abilities are powerful but have inherent limitations:
- Creature tokens are conditional (only when creatures die) and offset by needing creatures on board
- Artifact recursion is conditional and limited (artifacts must die, not be exiled or discarded)
- No combo potential—neither ability breaks out into infinite loops under reasonable circumstances

**Design Quality**
The card has a clear, focused purpose: reward attrition strategies. Both halves feed into this identity. It's not a kitchen sink because both abilities serve the same design goal (loss compensation). The abilities don't negate each other. No redundant conditionals are present.

**Custom Mechanics**
The card does not use any custom mechanics (Salvage, Malfunction, Overclock), so reminder text requirement is N/A.
