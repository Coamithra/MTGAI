**Analysis:**

This is a straightforward aggressive creature with excellent design for uncommon. Wasteland War-Raptor is a 3/3 haste trample for 4 mana that rewards go-wide strategies through its attack trigger.

**Templating & Mechanics:**
The oracle text is correctly templated. "Whenever ~ attacks, it gets +X/+0 until end of turn, where X is the number of other attacking creatures" is clean and unambiguous. The card uses no custom mechanics (Malfunction, Overclock, or Salvage), so reminder text is not required.

**Balance:**
At uncommon, a 3/3 haste trample for {2}{R}{R} is at rate or slightly above rate, comparable to cards like Raptor Companion (uncommon 2/2 for {1}{R}, plus similar abilities). The attack trigger provides meaningful upside but requires commitment—you need other attacking creatures to maximize the boost. A solo attack yields +0/+0 (no bonus), making the card solid but not dominant when you're light on creatures. With 2-3 other attackers, it becomes a 5-7/3 threat, which is powerful but requires investment and board state. The risk/reward is well-calibrated: the card incentivizes going wide without being oppressive if you can't.

**Design:**
The card has a focused purpose: rewarding aggressive, wide strategies. It's not a kitchen sink—just two evergreen keywords plus a meaningful combat trigger that plays into the set's WR (Spark Rebellion) and RG themes. The variability in power boost is real and meaningful; the bonus genuinely depends on your board state.

**Color Pie:**
Red haste and trample are evergreen for red. The bonus power effect is firmly in red's domain (temporary, combat-focused boosts). No color pie issues.

**Keyword Interactions:**
Haste and trample are both evergreen and have no conflicts here. The card doesn't negate any of its own abilities. No issues.

**No Custom Mechanics:**
This card doesn't use Malfunction, Overclock, or Salvage, so no reminder text is required.

**Variability:**
The +X/+0 boost varies meaningfully based on the number of other attacking creatures. This is real variability tied to board state, not a fixed hidden number.

The card is clean, well-balanced for uncommon, and has a focused design that fits the set's themes. No issues detected.
