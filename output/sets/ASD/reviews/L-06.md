### Salvage
- Type: keyword_ability
- Reminder text: (Look at the top X cards of your library. You may put an artifact card from among them into your hand. Put the rest on the bottom of your library in any order.)
- Colors: White, Blue, Green
- Complexity: 1
- Appears at: common, uncommon, rare, mythic
- Design notes: Primary mechanic for the set. Complexity 1 makes it common-viable. X scaling: 2-3 at common, 4-5 at uncommon, 6+ at rare/mythic. Renamed from 'Scavenge' to 'Salvage' to avoid collision with the existing MTG keyword 'Scavenge' from Return to Ravnica (which exiles creatures from graveyards to put +1/+1 counters on targets).

Overlaps well with WR (Spark Rebellion) and WU (Ancient Technology) draft archetypes. The colorless artifact slot (X-C-01) is a natural fit. Counter removal creates interesting interactions with the Cult of Science (UR) archetype.

### Overclock

- Colors: Blue, Red, Black
- Complexity: 3
- Appears at: uncommon, rare, mythic

High risk/reward: exiling 3 cards is significant, but playing them until end of turn is powerful. Fits UR (Mad Science) and UB (Deep Descent) archetypes. **Analysis:**

Descent Waypoint is a straightforward nonbasic dual land designed for the WU color pair in the set. It enters tapped and produces either white or blue mana, which is appropriate for a common fixing land in a 60-card set.

**Templating & Oracle Text:** The card follows standard MTG templating for lands. "Enters the battlefield tapped" is clear and correct. The mana ability {T}: Add {W} or {U} uses modern templating (choice templating rather than separate modes).

**Balance:** At common rarity, this is appropriately costed. Comparing to similar common fixing lands (e.g., basic dual lands from various sets that enter tapped), this is on-rate or slightly worse, which is correct for a nonbasic. The tempo cost of entering tapped is meaningful early game, and at common, that's an appropriate drawback.

**Design Quality:** The card has a focused purpose: provide color fixing for the WU draft archetype while applying a tempo penalty. It's not a kitchen sink card — it does one thing well. The flavor text ties it to the set's megadungeon theme nicely.

**Keyword Interactions:** There are no custom mechanics on this card, so no Salvage/Malfunction/Overclock interactions to check.

**Enters Tapped Relevance:** This is a land with a tap ability, so "enters tapped" is mechanically relevant — it delays the mana production by one turn. This is appropriate and not a nonbo.

**Color Pie:** White-Blue fixing is firmly within color pie. The colors match the design notes' intent.
