Kethra, Spark Commander is a well-designed rare legendary that avoids the pitfalls of overcosted or unfocused designs.

**Mechanics and templating:**
- Oracle text is clean and correctly templated. Three distinct abilities with clear mechanical interactions.
- No custom mechanics (Salvage, Malfunction, or Overclock) appear on this card, so reminder text requirements don't apply.
- No keyword negations: haste is fully functional (creature enters play untapped and can attack immediately).

**Balance:**
- A 2/2 haste for {1}{W}{R} is 3 mana total. On stats alone, this is below-rate (compare Goblin Warbands as a floor or similar 2/2 haste creatures). However, the card's power comes from its triggered ability and synergies, not raw stats. This is intentional and appropriate for a legendary with multiplied value.
- The token generation engine is the main payoff: every attack creates a 1/1 tapped attacking token. With protection and recursion in the format, this can generate significant value over several turns. However, the 2/2 body is fragile and needs time to generate tokens, and the tokens themselves are minimal (1/1s). This is adequately costed.
- The equip cost reduction is synergistic with the tokens, encouraging equipment-heavy lists. {1} equip costs are significantly cheaper than baseline, but the value is only realized if (a) you have equipment in play, (b) you have tokens to equip, and (c) you have the mana to equip. In typical games, this won't create runaway value instantly.
- Overall power level is fair for rare. Not pushed, not weak.

**Design Quality:**
- The card has a clear identity: token generation + equipment synergy + combat aggression. All three abilities work toward the same goal (making and powering up rebel tokens).
- Not a kitchen sink: three abilities, all synergistic. Each one supports the others.
- The design tells a story: "rebel leader makes small army, equips them quickly." This is cohesive.
- Color representation: White for tokens and equipment, Red for haste and combat damage. Both colors are pulling in the same direction. No color pie violations.

**Format fit:**
- The card is explicitly noted as a Spark Rebellion archetype leader (WR draft archetype), which makes sense given the haste + token + equipment synergy.
- Equipment synergy fits common Boros/Izzet themes in Magic.
- The malfunction/Salvage mechanics don't appear here, so no cross-mechanic issues.

**Potential concerns and verification:**
- "Whenever ~ attacks" triggers correctly with haste. The token enters "tapped and attacking" -- this is a corner case worth verifying. In Magic, "tapped" and "attacking" are distinct states. A tapped creature can be attacking (e.g., a creature that entered during combat and was declared as an attacker). The reminder text for the mechanic isn't needed here since this is not a custom mechanic, but the interaction is sound: the token enters the battlefield already declared as an attacker, tapped. This is valid in modern templating (see cards like Forbidden Friendship or Mogg War Marshal variants).
- The equip cost reduction only applies to equipment you control. This is correctly templated and avoids weird edge cases with opponent's equipment.

**Issues found:** None.

Kethra, Spark Commander is a well-designed rare legendary creature that serves as the primary commander for the Spark Rebellion (WR) archetype. Let me address the checklist:

**Templating & Oracle Text Correctness:** The oracle text is properly templated. "Whenever ~ attacks" correctly references the card in context, and the token creation text "create a 1/1 white Human Rebel creature token that's tapped and attacking" follows MTG conventions for simultaneous token properties.

**Custom Mechanics:** This card contains no custom mechanics (Salvage, Malfunction, or Overclock), so reminder text is not applicable.

Haste enables the first attack trigger immediately, making it functional and valuable.

**Conditional Logic:** No conditionals appear on this card.

**Power Level & Balance:** Kethra costs {1}{W}{R} (3 mana total) as a 2/2 with haste. At first glance, she's below-rate on raw stats (a 2/2 for 3 would typically have some drawback). However, the three abilities are genuinely synergistic and powerful in the intended archetype:
- Haste provides immediate board impact and triggers the token generator immediately
- Generates 1/1 token attackers on each attack, creating exponential board growth in a go-wide strategy
- Equipment cost reduction to {1} (from standard {2}) enables rapid equipping of the tokens and Kethra herself

In a dedicated equipment-focused WR deck, this creates a powerful engine. However, the 2/2 body is fragile—removal answers her efficiently, and without equipment or follow-up threats, she's vulnerable to blockers. The token generation requires combat, so she can be stalled. The power level is appropriate for rare in a focused archetype, similar to cards like **Goblin Rabblemaster** (which costs {1}{R} but has different mechanics) or **Puresteel Paladin** (which costs {1}{W} and is less aggressive but more efficient at equipping).

**Kitchen Sink Design:** This is NOT a kitchen sink card. All three abilities feed into a single cohesive strategy: "create rebel tokens, arm them with equipment, attack." The design is focused and intentional. Every ability supports the core gameplay loop.

**Variability:** Token generation is variable and meaningful—the number of tokens scales with the number of attacks, which depends on board state, deck composition, and gameplay decisions. This is real variability.

**Keyword Collision:** Haste is a standard MTG keyword with no collision. The card uses no custom mechanics.

**Enters Tapped Irrelevance:** The tokens "tapped and attacking" is relevant because they enter tapped, but as attackers in the current combat they're immediately committed. This is mechanically distinct and avoids them entering untapped (which would create a separate untap step value). The card itself doesn't have any enters-tapped clause.

**Color Pie:** White gets token generation and equipment synergy; Red gets haste and aggressive combat triggers. Both colors are well-represented and on-pie for their slice.

Overall, Kethra is a cohesive, well-balanced rare that tells a clear design story and enables the intended archetype without being overpowered in limited or constructed.
