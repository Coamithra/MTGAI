**Analysis:**

Scorched Passage is a straightforward common red instant that deals 4 damage to target creature. At {3}{R} (4 mana), this provides a clean removal spell at an appropriate rate for the rarity.

**Templating & Mechanics:** The oracle text is clean and correctly templated. No custom mechanics are present on this card, so reminder text requirements do not apply.

**Balance:** At common, 4 mana for 4 damage to a creature is fair. The card kills most common creatures but not everything (it won't take down a 5/5, for instance), which is appropriate for common removal. It's an instant, giving it flexibility in timing. Comparable cards: Flame Javelin deals 4 damage for {2}{R} at sorcery speed, but that card is from a different metagame. In the context of a 60-card dev set, this rate is reasonable—not exceptional, but solid. The design notes correctly note it doesn't hit players, keeping it pure creature removal rather than a reach spell, which is appropriate.

**Color Pie:** Red burn/direct damage is core to red's identity. This is entirely on-pie.

**Design Quality:** The card has a focused purpose (creature removal) with no extraneous effects. The flavor text is thematic and evocative. No kitchen sink issues.

**Keyword Interactions:** No keywords present; no interactions to worry about.

**Drawbacks:** The card's only real "drawback" is that it's instant-speed creature damage only (no player damage). This is intentional design to keep it as removal rather than reach, which is fine for common.

Overall, this is a clean, well-designed common removal spell with no issues.
