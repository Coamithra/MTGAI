**Keyword Negation**: Vigilance is NOT negated here. Vigilance allows the creature to attack without tapping, which is mechanically distinct from whether the creature "can" attack (a permission check vs. a tapping effect). The restriction "can't attack or block unless you control an artifact" is a separate permission layer that doesn't negate vigilance; rather, vigilance becomes more valuable when the condition IS met since the creature untaps for blocking. This is good design that rewards the deckbuilding constraint.

**Custom Mechanics**: Salvage is properly introduced with reminder text on first appearance. The reminder text is accurate to the specification provided.

**Conditional Meaningfulness**: The conditional "whenever an artifact enters under your control" is clearly meaningful — it only triggers when artifacts enter, which is not guaranteed and represents a real deckbuilding commitment. The death trigger's salvage is not conditional and always resolves.

**Power Level**: At {W}{W} with stats of 2/3, vigilance, and lifelink, Feretha appears reasonably balanced. Historical comparison: Anafenza, the Foremost was a 3-mana 4/3 with two major mechanics. Feretha costs 1 less mana but comes with a real restriction (can't attack/block without artifacts) that significantly constraints play patterns. The +1/+1 growth and death trigger salvage create value but require the artifact-heavy deckbuilding constraint to be meaningful. In an artifact-focused format like ASD, this is appropriate for mythic. The card is pushed but not broken — it demands support.

**Kitchen Sink**: This is NOT kitchen sink design. Feretha has three related mechanical axes: (1) artifact-conditional combat ability, (2) artifact-triggered growth, (3) death-trigger value. All three support the core theme of a creature dependent on artifacts for animation and worth, with the flavor reinforcing every mechanic. The purpose is laser-focused: create a build-around legendary for artifact decks.

**Variability**: The +1/+1 counter growth is variable based on how many artifacts enter during the game (real variability). The death trigger Salvage 4 is fixed at 4 but meaningfully different from "tutor 1 artifact" because you get to build choice into library ordering and selection.

**Keyword Collisions**: No collisions. Salvage is the custom keyword and has no existing MTG equivalent (Scavenge differs significantly). Vigilance and lifelink are existing keywords.

**Enters Tapped**: N/A — Feretha enters untapped and has no enters-tapped effects.

**Overall Assessment**: This is a well-executed mythic legendary that cohesively delivers on its design goals. It's flavorful, mechanically sound, and appropriately powered for its cost with meaningful restrictions. The artifact-dependence is not a bug but a feature that makes it a strong build-around but not format-warping.
