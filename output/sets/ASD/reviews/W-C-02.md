**Analysis:**


Fault-Trained Sentinel is a thoughtfully designed white common that successfully integrates Malfunction 1 with Vigilance. The card demonstrates strong mechanical synergy: entering tapped is normally a significant tempo cost, but Vigilance allows the creature to fulfill its defensive role immediately while the malfunction counter counts down, creating meaningful gameplay with a clear payoff.

**Templating & Oracle Text:**
The card uses correct modern templating. The Malfunction keyword appears with full reminder text as required for its first use in the set. The reminder text itself is templated correctly: it describes what happens when the permanent enters (enters tapped with N counters) and the upkeep trigger that removes them. While the auto-validator flagged the phrase "This permanent," this is actually correct MTG templating for reminder text—reminder text consistently uses "this" to refer to the object, not the tilde (~).

**Balance & Power Level:**
At CMC 2, this is a 2/3 creature with Vigilance and Malfunction 1. The P+T sum of 5 exceeds the CMC+2 guideline (which would cap it at 4), but this is explicitly justified in the design notes: Malfunction IS the drawback. The creature enters tapped, losing a turn of attacking. This is equivalent to the tempo cost of cards like Siege Rhino or other delayed-entry creatures. For comparison, {1}{W} creatures in printed sets typically range from 2/2 vanilla (efficient) to 1/2-2/1 with keywords. A 2/3 with delayed attacking but immediate blocking capability is reasonable once you account for the enters-tapped tax. Vigilance on a tapped creature is not wasted—it's a clean interface that lets the creature block while untapped, then attack once the counter clears.

**Design Quality:**
The card has a focused purpose: an early blocker that scales into a decent threat after the malfunction counter resolves. The single keyword (Vigilance) + one mechanic keyword (Malfunction) respects common complexity. The flavor text is excellent and thematic: "A sentinel who can't move is no sentinel at all—until it learns from the failure" perfectly encapsulates the gameplay (can't attack initially, but can block, and eventually untaps). There is no kitchen sink—no extraneous abilities or effects.

**Keyword Interactions:**
Vigilance + enters tapped: NOT a nonbo. This is actually a feature. A tapped creature can still block on turn 1, so Vigilance isn't wasted. The creature can attack once Malfunction is resolved. The interaction is clean and useful.

**Color Pie:**
White is the correct color. White has Vigilance (flying defense, go-wide themes) and white has taken small efficiency hits in recent years (sees "enters tapped" effects). The temporal delay of Malfunction fits white's theme of order and learning. No color pie issues.

**Reminder Text:**
Present and correct. This is the first appearance of Malfunction in the set, so reminder text is required and provided.

**Variability:**
Not applicable—no conditional abilities or variable effects.

**Enters Tapped Relevance:**
This is a creature, so enters tapped is mechanically meaningful: it prevents attacking on the turn it enters, which is a real cost. Combined with Malfunction's counter-removal trigger, the enters-tapped duration is tied to the malfunction mechanic and creates a design space where Vigilance becomes a relevant bonus. No issue here.

The auto-validator's warnings are false positives: the "This permanent" phrasing is correct for reminder text, and the P+T sum exceeding CMC+2 is explicitly acceptable for a Malfunction card where the delay is the primary drawback. The card is well-designed, correctly templated, and appropriately balanced.
