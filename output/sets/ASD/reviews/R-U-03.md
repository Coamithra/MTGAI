**Analysis:**

Combustion Cascade is a red uncommon with solid design intent—it creates a meaningful turn-of-overclocking payoff where the 2 damage triggers reward a full turn of spell casting. The complexity argument (Overclock is tertiary, complexity 3) is defensible, and the first-use reminder text is appropriately included.

However, there are two critical issues:

**1. Overclock at Uncommon (Rules Violation)**
The custom mechanic specification explicitly states: "Tertiary mechanic, rare+ only (complexity 3). Should NOT appear at common." This card violates that design constraint. While the reminder text makes the mechanic clear, the design notes justify this violation weakly. Overclock at uncommon sets a dangerous precedent for a tertiary mechanic and likely extends beyond the intended power band for the rarity. This needs to be elevated to rare, or the card needs to be redesigned to use a simpler mechanic.

**2. The "if you overclocked this turn" Conditional is Redundant**
Since Overclock on entry is mandatory (as an ETB trigger), the condition "if you overclocked this turn" is always true when the second ability can trigger. The condition cannot fail in normal play—you will always have overclocked that turn. This makes the conditional ornamental rather than meaningful, which is poor templating.

**Suggested fixes:**
- Move the card to rare rarity, or
- Redesign the second ability to remove the redundant conditional (e.g., "Whenever you cast a noncreature spell, ~ deals 2 damage to each opponent"), or
- Use a different trigger structure that makes the conditional actually variable (e.g., "If Overclock was cast as a spell this turn" or similar).

The card is not fundamentally broken—the damage output is reasonable—but it violates set constraints and has unnecessary conditional text.

- **[FAIL] other:** Overclock is a tertiary mechanic (complexity 3) designed for rare+ only, but appears here at uncommon, violating the design specification.
- **[WARN] redundant_conditional:** The 'if you overclocked this turn' clause is redundant because Overclock enters tapped with a mandatory ETB trigger, guaranteeing the condition is always true when the second ability can trigger.
