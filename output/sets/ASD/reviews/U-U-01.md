**Analysis:**

Flickerfield Operative is a clean blue 1-drop uncommon with scry 2 on entry. The card is straightforward in design and execution: a 1/1 body paired with a cantrip-like effect. This is well within blue's slice of the color pie (selection and information advantage). 

Comparing to printed precedent: Scry as a standalone mechanic at common/uncommon (like Opt) costs 1U for the spell alone. Here we get it stapled to a 1/1 creature for U, which is definitely above the rate of a bare scry-only card, but the 1/1 body is fragile and scry 2 (not scry 1, not draw) is limited. The card is reasonably costed for uncommon without being oppressive. It supports blue's broader strategy in the limited environment while serving as an enabler for artifact-focused synergies (helping find key pieces).

Templating is correct: "When ~ enters, scry 2" uses modern wording (ETB trigger with basic action). No custom mechanics appear, so reminder text requirements don't apply. Power level is slightly above common but appropriate for uncommon — comparable to printed cards like Portent or similar cantrip-creatures.

No nonbos or mechanical conflicts. No kitchen sink design (single coherent effect). The card does exactly what it says.
