**Analysis:**


Automated Sentry Grid is a well-designed blue uncommon enchantment that functions as a defensive pillow-fort piece. Let me work through the checklist:

**Templating & Oracle Text:** The card uses clean modern templating. "Whenever a creature an opponent controls attacks, tap it" is standard. The follow-up "It doesn't untap during its controller's next untap step unless its controller pays {2}" is correctly templated and clearly states the conditional untap cost. The second ability "Whenever an artifact enters under your control, scry 1" is also correctly formatted. No custom mechanics (Salvage, Malfunction, Overclock) appear on this card, so reminder text is not applicable.

**Keyword Interactions:** No keywords are negated or made useless here. This is not a creature, so the tap mechanic has full mechanical weight.

**Conditional Meaningfulness:** The conditional "unless its controller pays {2}" is meaningful and non-redundant. An opponent can choose to pay {2} to untap the creature, creating real decision points and preventing total lockdown. This is good design.

**Power Level:** At CMC 5 (3UU), this card provides valuable late-game stabilization. Comparable cards: Frost Titan (5 mana, creature, taps on attack) was a strong blue card in its Standard environment. Dungeon Geists (4 mana, creature with flying) also taps creatures. This is an enchantment (not a creature, so no combat stats), and it applies to ALL attacking creatures, not just one per turn. The scry rider adds utility. The {2} opt-out prevents infinite lockdown. At uncommon, this feels appropriately powerful for a late-game stabilizer without being oppressive. It's above a typical utility enchantment but below pushed, which is appropriate for this role at 5 mana.

**Kitchen Sink:** The card has two abilities: (1) tap all attacking creatures and prevent untapping unless paid for, and (2) scry 1 when artifacts enter. Both tie to the set's artifact theme and defense strategy. They're related in purpose (late-game stabilization) rather than unrelated effects. This is not kitchen sink design. The scry rider is a minor bonus that fits the "ancient technology" flavor.

**Variability:** Both abilities are straightforward with no false variability. There's no counting or conditional scaling.

**Mechanic Name Collisions:** No custom mechanics appear on this card, so no collision issues.

**Enters Tapped:** Not applicable; this is not a permanent that enters the battlefield with tapped status.

**Color Pie:** This is solidly in blue's pie. Tapping creatures is a blue staple (or blue-adjacent in some cases, but blue has always had this through control effects). Scry is a core blue mechanic. The defensive posture is very much blue. No pie concerns.

**Set Context:** This card fits well into the "Anomalous Descent" set's artifact subtheme and would pair nicely with salvage mechanics and other artifact generators, creating a cohesive limited strategy.

Overall assessment: This is a well-executed uncommon that serves a clear purpose without overextending. No issues detected.
