**Analysis:**

Rendon Apex Tracker is a well-designed green uncommon that presents a focused, synergistic package. At CMC 4, the 4/3 trample statline is appropriately costed—slightly above a vanilla 4/3 for 3G (which would be underpowered), but the trample evasion and the combat-triggered tutor elevate the card meaningfully. This tracks closely with cards like Courser of Kruphix (3/2 that also draws/tutors but without the trample) and Elvish Archer (2/2 with a different activation), making the overall power level sound for uncommon.

The card's core design is elegant: trample naturally synergizes with a combat damage trigger, ensuring the trigger can reliably fire and rewarding the player for connecting. The creature-filtering tutor (distinct from Salvage, which tutors artifacts) supports green's creature-centric gameplan and fills a meaningful niche—green should have access to threat tutoring, especially when it requires a combat condition.

Oracle text follows modern MTG templating correctly: "Whenever ~ deals combat damage to a player, look at the top three cards of your library. You may put a creature card from among them into your hand. Put the rest on the bottom of your library in any order." This mirrors the structure of cards like Abundant Harvest and uses the "look at X, may put one into hand, put the rest on bottom in any order" template accurately.

Trample and the combat trigger are not in conflict—trample makes the trigger more likely to resolve, which is intentional design synergy, not a nonbo. The combat-damage condition is meaningful and cannot always be guaranteed, so the tutor is properly gated.

No custom mechanics (Salvage, Malfunction, Overclock) appear on this card, so reminder text is not applicable. There are no keyword collisions, no enters-tapped irrelevancies, no kitchen sink elements, and no redundant conditionals. The card is color-pie appropriate for green (creature tutoring, trample, combat-centric value).
