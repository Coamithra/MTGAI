**Analysis:**

Salvage Beetle is a well-designed uncommon green artifact creature that integrates cleanly into the Anomalous Descent set's artifact-matters theme. 

**Templating & Mechanics**: The oracle text is correctly templated. Reach is an evergreen green keyword appropriate to the color, and the triggered ability "Whenever an artifact you control is put into a graveyard from the battlefield, put a +1/+1 counter on ~" is clearly worded and mechanically sound. The card does not use any custom mechanics, so no reminder text is required.

**Color Pie**: The card is entirely within green's domain. Green has reach (creature evasion/combat control), and the artifact-sacrifice synergy fits green's resource-and-land themes. The creature type (Insect Beast) is appropriate for green in a post-apocalyptic setting.

**Balance**: At {1}{G} for a 2/2 with reach, Salvage Beetle is on-curve for a common/uncommon green creature. The triggered ability provides meaningful growth potential but is not guaranteed — the payoff depends on actual artifact sacrifices or destruction in play. This is not a free effect; it requires setup and synergy with other cards in the environment. Comparable cards like Zulaport Cutthroat or Viscera Seer show that sacrifice-payoff creatures at low costs are acceptable at uncommon when the condition requires deckbuilding support. No balance flag.

**Design Quality**: The card has a focused, cohesive identity: a small creature that grows through artifact loss. This ties directly into the set's artifact subtheme and reward players who run artifact-heavy sacrifice strategies. The triggered ability creates real variability — it will trigger an unpredictable number of times depending on how many artifacts are sacrificed or destroyed in a game. There is no artificial randomness or fake conditions. The design respects the "no kitchen sink" principle: reach + growth trigger is two related mechanical facets serving one purpose.

**Keyword Interactions**: Reach is not negated or rendered useless by the triggered ability. A 2/2 flyer would be stronger than a 2/2 with reach, so reach + growth potential is coherent without redundancy or nonbos.

**Variability**: The variability is genuinely real. The number of times the triggered ability resolves depends on the actual artifacts you control being put into a graveyard, which varies by game state and matchup. This is not a fake-count or always-fixed scenario.

**Flavor**: The flavor text ties the mechanical identity to world-building. The growth-through-decay theme mirrors the mechanical reality and reinforces the post-apocalyptic renewal setting.

The card is well-executed with no significant issues.
