## Original Card

> Name: Anomalous Resonance
> Mana Cost: {2}{U}{U}
> Type: Enchantment
> Rarity: rare
> Oracle Text: Whenever an artifact enters under your control, you may draw a card. If you do, discard a card.
> At the beginning of your end step, if three or more artifacts entered under your control this turn, draw two cards.
> Flavor Text: The dungeon hums at a frequency only the desperate can hear.
> Design Notes: A blue rare enchantment that rewards artifact-heavy strategies. The first ability is a loot trigger — each artifact entering gives you card filtering, keeping hands smooth without raw card advantage. The second ability is the payoff: if you can get three artifacts entering in a single turn (via tokens, cheap artifacts, or Salvage chains), you draw two cards at end step — genuine card advantage. The threshold of three artifacts is achievable but requires real deckbuilding commitment. At 4 CMC it comes down early enough to matter but isn't free. Supports the WU and UR artifact archetypes as a build-around. Blue is primary in both card draw and enchantments-that-draw.

**Anomalous Resonance** is a blue rare enchantment with a tightly focused artifact-synergy theme that fits well into the set's mechanical ecosystem. Here's my detailed assessment:

**Oracle Text & Templating:**
The card uses modern, clean templating. Both abilities are straightforward:
1. "Whenever an artifact enters under your control, you may draw a card. If you do, discard a card." — This is loot templating, correctly phrased.
2. "At the beginning of your end step, if three or more artifacts entered under your control this turn, draw two cards." — Proper conditional phrasing and timing.

No custom mechanics appear on this card, so reminder text is not applicable. The card is clear and rules-legal.

**Balance:**
At {2}{U}{U} (4 CMC), this card sits at a reasonable rate for a blue rare enchantment. Comparing to printed equivalents:
- **Talrand, Sky Summoner** (3 CMC) generates tokens and draws cards, also a 4-mana commitment when cast from hand.
- **Chasm Skulker** (2 CMC) filters and evasion, also reaches 4 mana.
- **Glazier Sphinx** (4 CMC) is pure card advantage in a different color.

The first ability (conditional loot trigger) provides consistent smoothing but not raw card advantage—each artifact entering cycles you through your deck at the cost of hand disruption. This is appropriate for uncommon-rate effects. The second ability (draw two cards at end step for three artifact entries) is the legitimate payoff. It requires real deckbuilding commitment (three artifact entries in one turn) and comes as a reward at a specific point.

This is fairly costed. The payoff is real but requires setup, and the threshold of three artifacts is meaningful in a limited format without being trivial. The loot trigger prevents the enchantment from being a dead draw in non-artifact heavy hands, adding flexibility.

**Design Quality:**
The card has a **focused purpose**: reward artifact-heavy strategies with card advantage. Both abilities pull in the same direction (rewarding artifacts entering), and the mechanics create an interesting decision tree:
- Do I enable the loot trigger even though it costs a card from hand?
- Can I engineer three artifact entries this turn for the bigger payoff?
- How many artifacts is "enough" for this to be worth the slot?

This is excellent limited-focused design. The card is **not a kitchen sink**—it does one thing (artifact synergy) with two complementary mechanisms (loot feedback + threshold payoff).

**Variability:**
The variability is **real**:
- The loot trigger fires once per artifact entry, but only if you choose to and have cards to draw.
- The end-step draw is conditional: it only triggers if you hit the three-artifact threshold, which is not guaranteed. Some turns you'll get no payoff; good turns you'll draw two cards.
- The number of times the loot trigger fires varies based on how many artifacts enter (0–many).

The condition "three or more artifacts entered" is not redundant—it can genuinely fail to trigger if you play, say, only one or two artifacts, or if the artifacts are stalled by removal.

**Color Pie:**
Blue is the primary color for card draw and enchantments-that-draw (e.g., **Rhystic Study**, **Necropotence**, **Future Sight**). The loot mechanic is solidly in blue's wheelhouse (filtering). The artifact synergy is colorless/pan-blue, fitting UW and UR themes. No color pie issues.
