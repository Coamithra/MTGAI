**Analysis:**

Sanctioned Exile is a clean, well-designed common removal spell. The card presents a simple conditional exile effect on an instant, which is entirely within white's color pie. At three mana, targeting only creatures with power 4 or greater, it occupies an appropriate niche in white's removal suite: handling large threats while being unable to deal with small utility creatures or tokens. This conditional restriction is precisely aligned with New World Order (NWO) common removal guidelines, which requires common removal to have meaningful restrictions.

Comparing to established precedents: Reprisal (2W instant, exile target creature with flying) was printed at common in Tempest, and Pacifism effects have always been common staples. Sanctioned Exile is more restrictive than pure unconditional exile would be, but it's unrestricted by card type or other properties beyond power level, making it a reasonable middle ground. Instant speed is appropriate and adds player agency without being a significant power premium for the effect.

The card's templating is correct and concise. There are no custom mechanics present (Salvage, Malfunction, Overclock), so no reminder text is needed. The power level is appropriate for common: it solves a specific problem (large threats) while leaving other removal needs to other cards. The card has a single, focused purpose with no kitchen-sink design elements. The flavor ties cleanly to the set's post-apocalyptic megadungeon theme with the exiling of undesirables into the subsurface.

There are no issues with keyword interactions, color pie adherence, or card mechanics. This is a straightforward, well-executed removal card suitable for its rarity and mana cost.
