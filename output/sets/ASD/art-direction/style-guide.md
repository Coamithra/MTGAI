# Anomalous Descent (ASD) — Art Style Guide

## Overall Artistic Direction

**Style**: Stylized digital illustration. Bold shapes, strong silhouettes, clean composition with painterly texture. Not photorealistic — slightly exaggerated proportions and saturated color. Prioritizes readability at card size (small art box). Think concept art meets graphic novel: strong linework implied through value contrast, not literal outlines.

**Tone**: Played completely straight. The subjects are bizarre (20-foot robot bank guards, wizards in flying stone heads, clockwork cats) but the art treats them with the same compositional gravitas as any serious fantasy illustration. No winking at the camera. No cartoonish exaggeration. The deadpan absurdity lives in the *what*, not the *how it's rendered*.

**Visual Influences**:
- Slay the Spire / Hades (stylized game art with strong shapes and atmosphere)
- Moebius (surreal landscapes, intricate world-building in single frames)
- Classic MTG concept art (Chippy, Noah Bradley compositions — dramatic framing)
- Retro sci-fi poster art (bold silhouettes against atmospheric backdrops)
- 1E D&D interior art spirit (gritty, lived-in, things-have-been-here-a-long-time)

**What to Avoid**:
- Photorealism or hyper-detail that muddies at card size
- Cartoon/chibi/anime aesthetics
- Clean, sterile sci-fi (Star Trek bridge aesthetic)
- Generic medieval fantasy with no tech/ruin elements
- Bright, cheerful color palettes — even white cards should feel dusty and weathered
- AI art "tells": symmetrical faces, overly smooth skin, nonsensical background details

## World Setting

**Geography**: Far-future post-apocalyptic Earth. The surface is a patchwork of wilderness (forests, scrubland, rocky badlands) dotted with crumbling ruins of a hyper-advanced civilization. The city of Denethix sits at the edge of the wilderness near Mount Rendon, beneath which lies the Anomalous Subsurface Environment — a self-spawning megadungeon predating humanity.

**Two Worlds**: The set has a clear above/below duality:
- **Surface (Denethix & Wilderness)**: Warm, dusty, sun-baked. A medieval-ish city built in and around ancient mega-structures. Dinosaurs roam outside the walls. Wizards inhabit crumbling towers. The Unyielding Fist patrols with laser rifles.
- **Dungeon (The ASE)**: Cool, eerie, vast. Impossible architecture. Ancient machinery humming in the dark. Bioluminescent fungus. Things that shouldn't exist. The deeper you go, the weirder it gets.

**Architecture**: Denethix is layered — crude medieval construction (wood, stone, thatch) built atop or inside massive ancient structures (concrete, steel, glass). Exposed rebar. Repurposed ventilation shafts as alleyways. The Bank Inviolable is a fortress of smooth black metal guarded by towering robot sentinels. The Cult of Science headquarters has exposed circuitry and flickering holographic displays.

**Technology Level**: Post-collapse. Ancient super-science relics exist but are barely understood. Laser weapons, force fields, protonium reactors — all treated as mystical artifacts. New technology is crude: torches, swords, crossbows. The contrast between ancient sleek tech and crude medieval survival is a core visual motif.

**Flora**: Surface vegetation is hardy and overgrown — thick moss on ancient concrete, vines cracking through steel plating, twisted trees growing from rooftop gardens. In the dungeon: bioluminescent fungi (blue-green, violet), crystalline growths, the occasional impossible garden lit by ancient grow-lights.

**Fauna**: Dinosaurs in the wilderness (raptors, pterosaurs, massive herbivores), giant insects, moktars (tribal humanoids), screechmen, grunkies. In the dungeon: degraded automatons, morlocks, oozes, constructs, and things with no name.

## Color Palette

### Master Palette

**Primary tones**: Amber, ochre, warm grey (surface); steel blue, deep teal, violet (dungeon)
**Accent colors**: Laser-green (#39FF14), protonium gold (#FFD700), eldritch purple (#7B2D8E), rust orange (#C1440E)
**Neutrals**: Sandstone (#D4C5A9), concrete grey (#8A8D8F), deep shadow (#1A1A2E)

The palette skews warm and dusty on the surface, cold and eerie underground. Ancient technology glows — lasers are vivid green, protonium emits warm gold, eldritch energy pulses purple. These accent colors should pop against the muted base tones.

### Per-Color Identity

**White** — *Order, Civilization, the Unyielding Fist*
- Dominant tones: Sandstone, ivory, warm gold, polished bronze
- Lighting: Harsh midday sun, long sharp shadows, dust motes in light shafts
- Motifs: Denethix city walls, uniformed soldiers, laser rifles with gold glow, ancient architecture repurposed as government buildings, stone carvings of Feretha
- Texture: Sun-bleached stone, clean metal (the Fist keeps their gear polished), parchment, linen
- Mood: Authoritative, ordered, quietly oppressive

**Blue** — *Knowledge, Ancient Tech, the Cult of Science*
- Dominant tones: Steel blue, teal, cool silver, holographic cyan
- Lighting: Screen-glow, holographic projections, the cold light of ancient displays in dark rooms
- Motifs: Circuit patterns, glass domes, protonium devices, laboratory equipment, the ASE's deeper chambers, data crystals, the Head Scientist's prosthetics
- Texture: Smooth metal, glass, liquid crystal, cold condensation on ancient pipes
- Mood: Clinical, curious, slightly unsettling — poking at things you don't understand

**Black** — *Secrets, Decay, the Deep Dungeon*
- Dominant tones: Deep purple, sickly green-black, charcoal, dried-blood red
- Lighting: Minimal — flickering torches, the glow of dying machinery, bioluminescent decay
- Motifs: Feretha's brain-machine, the Vizier's hidden chambers, morlocks, undead automatons, the House of Flesh, things in the dark with too many eyes
- Texture: Corroded metal, organic-machine fusion, wet stone, viscera, tarnished bronze
- Mood: Dread, secrets, rot beneath a polished surface

**Red** — *Chaos, Fire, Raiders & Rebellion*
- Dominant tones: Rust orange, furnace red, hot amber, spark-white
- Lighting: Firelight, explosions, forge-glow, the flash of laser discharge
- Motifs: Moktar war parties, bandit camps, the Luminous Spark's raids, overclocked machinery sparking and smoking, dinosaur rampages, shattered automatons
- Texture: Rust, scorched metal, rough leather, cracked earth, soot
- Mood: Volatile, dangerous, freedom through destruction

**Green** — *Wilderness, Growth, Reclamation*
- Dominant tones: Deep emerald, moss, amber-green, fungal blue-green
- Lighting: Dappled forest light, bioluminescent glow, the warm amber of sunset over the frontier
- Motifs: Vines cracking through ancient structures, dinosaurs grazing, frontier settlements, salvaged tech repurposed as tools, fungal networks, the wilderness slowly eating civilization
- Texture: Bark, moss, chitin, rough-hewn wood, vine-wrapped steel
- Mood: Patient, primal, the world reclaiming what was taken

**Multicolor/Gold** — Combines the palettes of its constituent colors. Gold frame cards should feature scenes where two worlds collide (e.g., a soldier using salvaged tech in the wilderness, a scientist communing with dungeon fungi).

**Artifact/Colorless** — *Ancient Relics, Pure Technology*
- Dominant tones: Gunmetal, chrome, protonium gold accents, cool neutral grey
- Lighting: Self-illuminated — ancient tech has its own internal glow
- Motifs: Protonium devices, God's Eye, Fereyn's Stone Head, automatons, the things the dungeon built
- Texture: Brushed metal, glass, crystalline structures, machined precision degraded by millennia
- Mood: Alien, ancient, indifferent to humanity

**Land** — *Places of Power*
- Panoramic compositions, no focal characters
- The surface lands emphasize the scale differential: tiny medieval structures against massive ancient megastructures
- Dungeon lands emphasize depth and the unknown — vast dark chambers with distant pinpoints of light
- Basic lands show the dual nature: a forest with steel girders for tree trunks, a mountain that's half natural rock and half exposed bunker

## Mood & Atmosphere

**Emotional Register by Rarity**:
- **Common**: Everyday life in a strange world. A soldier on patrol. A scavenger prying open a panel. A dinosaur eating. Grounded, slice-of-life-in-the-post-apocalypse.
- **Uncommon**: Heightened tension or wonder. A dungeon chamber with something wrong about it. A confrontation between factions. Technology activating unexpectedly.
- **Rare**: Dramatic set-pieces. The full council of the Cult of Science. A moktar warband charging. The first sight of an impossible dungeon chamber. Named characters in defining moments.
- **Mythic**: Awe and dread. Feretha's brain pulsing in its machine. Fereyn's stone head soaring over Denethix. The ASE itself — vast, alien, alive. The scale should make the viewer feel small.

**Weather & Atmosphere**:
- Surface: Dusty haze, long shadows, a sun that feels slightly too red/orange (dying Earth). Occasional storms with unnatural lightning (green, purple).
- Dungeon: Still air, condensation, the hum of ancient machinery. Occasional gusts from ventilation systems that haven't been maintained in millennia.

## Recurring Visual Motifs

These elements should appear across multiple cards to create visual cohesion:

1. **The Red Sun** — The sky is never quite the right color. Sunsets are deep crimson. Midday light has an amber cast. This unifies all surface scenes.
2. **Layered Architecture** — Medieval crude construction on/in/around ancient mega-structures. Visible in backgrounds even when not the focus.
3. **The Glow** — Ancient tech always has internal illumination. Green for weapons/active systems, gold for protonium/power sources, purple for eldritch/dungeon-spawned things. This glow should appear in every card that features technology.
4. **Overgrowth** — Nature reclaiming technology. Vines on metal, fungi on circuits, roots cracking concrete. Even in Denethix, nature is winning slowly.
5. **Scale Disparity** — Humans are small against the ancient world. Doorways built for something bigger. Corridors too wide. Ceilings too high. The dungeon wasn't built for us.
6. **Feretha's Symbol** — A stylized face/mask motif appearing on government buildings, soldier armor, official documents. The face of a dead man maintaining order.

## Artist Style Personas (Per Color)

Each color gets a distinct artistic sub-style within the shared stylized-digital umbrella. This creates visual variety (simulating different "artists" in the set) while maintaining cohesion through the shared palette, world-building, and motifs above. The art prompt pipeline reads `card.colors` and applies the matching persona.

**White — "Classical Authority"**
- Structured, balanced compositions with strong vertical lines
- Clean edges, warm sandstone tones, golden-hour lighting
- Almost propaganda-poster framing — subjects centered, dignified, imposing
- Influence: Greg Rutkowski's architectural scenes, classical oil painting compositions
- *The Unyielding Fist wants you to see order. The art obliges.*

**Blue — "Clinical Futurism"**
- Geometric compositions, cooler palette, precise details
- Screen-glow and holographic lighting, reflective surfaces
- Influence: Moebius environments, Syd Mead industrial design, science illustration
- Slightly flatter color with sharp value contrasts — the most "graphic" style
- *The Cult of Science sees the world as a diagram. The art agrees.*

**Black — "Dark Grotesque"**
- Heavy use of shadow (60%+ of frame can be dark), minimal light sources
- Organic textures, wet surfaces, unsettling asymmetry
- Influence: Beksiński's biomechanical horror, Caravaggio's chiaroscuro
- The most painterly and textural of the styles — visible brushwork in shadows
- *The dungeon doesn't want to be seen clearly. The art respects that.*

**Red — "Dynamic Action"**
- Tilted angles, diagonal compositions, implied motion
- High contrast, warm fire-tones, sparks and debris
- Influence: action movie cinematography, Frank Frazetta's kinetic energy
- The most saturated and contrasty style — everything feels mid-explosion
- *Raiders don't pose. The art catches them in motion.*

**Green — "Naturalist Exploration"**
- Wider vistas, more environmental context, dappled light
- Rich earth tones, layered depth (foreground foliage framing)
- Influence: Hudson River School landscapes meets sci-fi concept art
- The most painterly and atmospheric — soft edges, rich color gradients
- *The wilderness doesn't care about your dungeon. The art shows why.*

**Artifact/Colorless — "Technical Precision"**
- Centered, object-focused compositions against dark/neutral backgrounds
- Self-illuminated subjects with internal glow (protonium gold, circuitry green)
- Influence: product photography meets archaeological illustration
- Clean negative space, the subject IS the composition
- *Ancient relics present themselves. The art steps back.*

**Multicolor — "Style Collision"**
- Blends the personas of its constituent colors
- WB (Vizier's Regime): Classical Authority + Dark Grotesque = *dignified corruption*
- UR (Mad Science): Clinical Futurism + Dynamic Action = *experiments going wrong, beautifully*
- BG (Twisted Evolution): Dark Grotesque + Naturalist Exploration = *nature reclaiming through horror*
- The blend should feel intentional, not muddled — pick the dominant color's composition style, add the secondary's palette/lighting

**Land — "Panoramic Wonder"**
- Widest compositions, no focal characters, pure environment
- Style matches the land's color identity but at landscape scale
- Dramatic atmospheric perspective — foreground detail fading to hazy distance
- The most "concept art" feeling — establishing shots for the world

## Creature & Character Design Principles

**Humans**: Practical, worn clothing. Leather, rough-spun fabric, repurposed ancient materials. The Unyielding Fist wears matching uniforms with ancient-tech accessories (laser holsters, comm devices they don't fully understand). The Cult of Science wears lab coats over improvised protective gear. Common people look medieval-scavenger.

**Automatons/Constructs**: The key design principle is *degraded grandeur*. These were once sleek, precise machines. Now they're dented, patched, jury-rigged. Exposed wiring. Mismatched replacement parts. They still function, but barely, and wrong. Malfunction counters should be visually represented: sparks, flickering lights, stuttering movement.

**Moktars**: Tribal, muscular humanoids. Bone/tooth jewelry, scavenged tech worn as trophies. War paint. They exist between worlds — savage but not stupid.

**Dungeon Creatures**: The deeper you go, the less things look like they should exist. Surface creatures are recognizable (dinosaurs, spiders, goblins). Deep dungeon creatures are *wrong* — hybrid forms, impossible biology, things the dungeon assembled from spare parts. Think the Sasquatron: bigfoot body + crab claw arm + glass dome machinery head. Played straight.

**Legendary Characters** (each needs a distinct visual identity):
- **Feretha, the Undying**: Dead wizard, brain in a machine. Cables, fluid tubes, the golden glow of ancient preservation tech. His "throne room" is a server room.
- **Koyl Yrenum, the Vizier**: Immaculately dressed for the setting. Dark robes with subtle ancient-tech embroidery. Sharp eyes. The most put-together person in Denethix.
- **Captain Marcus Tyro**: Battle-scarred, practical. Unyielding Fist uniform with laser rifle. The pragmatic soldier archetype.
- **Fereyn, Head in the Clouds**: A wizard literally inside a giant floating stone head. Eye-lasers. This is absurd and the art must treat it as majestic.
- **Monsator, the Reaper**: Mounted warrior commanding cornstalk warriors. Agricultural horror played as military might.
- **The Head Scientist**: 12-foot lab coat concealing stilts. Prosthetic limbs. Multiple tool-arms. A walking laboratory.
- **Terrible Karak**: Bandit king. Scarred, loud, draped in stolen finery and scavenged tech trophies.
- **Jace, Anomaly Seeker**: The outsider. Clean, modern, distinctly *not from here*. His planeswalker aesthetic should contrast sharply with the world's grime.

## Composition Guidelines for Card Art

**Focal Point**: Center-upper third of the frame. This is where the eye goes at card size. Place the subject's face/key feature here.

**Safe Zones**: The top 10% may be partially obscured by the card name bar. The bottom 15% may be partially obscured by the type line. Keep the most critical visual information in the central 75%.

**Aspect Ratio**: Art box is approximately 1.37:1 landscape. Generate at 1536x1120 or higher.

**Readability Test**: If you squint and the subject is still identifiable as a distinct silhouette, the composition works. If it becomes a muddy blur, simplify.

**Background Depth**: Use atmospheric perspective — backgrounds should be hazier, less saturated, less detailed than foregrounds. This is critical for dungeon scenes where dark-on-dark can lose depth.

## Mechanic Visual Language

**Salvage** (W/U/G): The moment of discovery. Hands reaching into wreckage. A panel pried open to reveal glowing circuitry. The art should show the *finding*, not the *using*. Warm metallic tones — bronze, copper, gold — for the salvaged item, contrasting with the decay around it.

**Malfunction** (W/U/R): Sparks, stuttering movement, flickering indicators. Machines that are powerful but unreliable. Visual shorthand: cracked glass, exposed wiring with visible sparks, status lights showing red/amber instead of green. The art should convey "this is about to work... or explode."

**Overclock** (U/R/B): Overheating, redlining, burning bright. Machinery pushed past safe limits — glowing cherry-red, steam venting, containment fields flickering. The art should feel dangerous and exhilarating. More saturated, more contrast than normal. The subject is briefly magnificent and possibly about to die.

## Technical Specifications

- **Resolution**: Minimum 1536x1120px, ideal 2048x1496px
- **Color Space**: sRGB
- **Format**: PNG (lossless)
- **Print Target**: 300 DPI on final card (822x1122px card, ~580x420px art box)
