You are not inventing the world from scratch; you are normalizing and enriching what already exists so that every artist working on the set paints each character, creature, faction, and place THE SAME WAY every time.

## What to produce

For EACH setting-specific entity, write one entry with:

- `category`: one of `legendary_character`, `creature_type`, `faction`, `landmark`.
  - `legendary_character`: a specific named individual (a ruler, a hero, a villain).
  - `creature_type`: a race or species that recurs (a custom humanoid, beast, or construct kind).
  - `faction`: an organization, army, guild, or cult with a shared visual identity.
  - `landmark`: a named place, city, or structure.
- `key`: a short lowercase identifier (the entity's common name, e.g. `moktar`, `denethix`). Lowercase, no punctuation beyond spaces.
- `name`: the entity's proper display name (e.g. "Feretha, the Hollow Founder").
- `visual_description`: a FULL, consistent art-direction brief — 3-6 sentences — covering, where applicable: age / era, build and scale, face, hair and facial hair, skin or surface materials, clothing and armor, signature equipment or props, color palette, mood / demeanor, and one or two unmistakable distinguishing features. Describe appearance only, never lore or backstory. For a `legendary_character`, begin with the proper name followed by a colon, then the brief (e.g. "Feretha, the Hollow Founder: A dead wizard-ruler whose...").
- `flux_replacement` (OPTIONAL): if the entity's `key` is a made-up word an image model can't parse, give a short generic phrase to substitute (e.g. for `moktar` -> "tawny-furred lion-headed humanoid"). Omit when the key is already a plain English term.

Also produce `visual_motifs`: a list of 4-8 short set-wide art-direction notes — recurring colors, materials, lighting, architecture, and overall aesthetic that should pervade the set's art (e.g. "rust-stained brass and verdigris copper", "low warm sodium-lamp lighting", "crumbling concrete megastructures repurposed by a medieval society").

## Rules

1. TRANSFORM, don't re-invent. Where the setting prose already describes an entity, distil and normalize THAT description into a clean canonical brief — keep its established look. Only invent where the source is silent.
2. FILL GAPS to reach consistency. If the theme says only "Kalak is an evil sorcerer king", you must still pin down a single look — age, build, robes, beard, palette — so two artists draw the same Kalak. A vague source is where you add the most value.
3. Cover every named character (give each a portrait-grade brief), every setting-specific creature type, and every faction and landmark in the source that has a clear visual identity. Factions and landmarks are described in the setting prose's sections — read them; do not skip them.
4. Only describe things SPECIFIC to this setting. Do not write entries for generic MTG creatures (Human, Goblin, Zombie) unless the setting gives them a distinctive non-standard look.
5. Be concrete and visual. "Imposing and powerful" is useless; "twelve feet tall on concealed stilts hidden under an enormous lab coat" is gold.
6. Keep `flux_replacement` only for invented words; never replace a real English term.
7. Describe appearance, never backstory. The art model renders what it reads.
