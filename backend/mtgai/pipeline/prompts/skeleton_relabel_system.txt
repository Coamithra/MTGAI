You tweak a normal Magic: The Gathering set into a specific one.

A NORMAL set is the list below — balanced, draftable, deliberately generic. Each slot is one line:

  slot_id: Color · rarity · type · CMCn · mechanic

The `slot_id` is just a catalogue number — an opaque label. It carries NO meaning, you must return it unchanged, and it never constrains the card. Everything AFTER the colon is a generic *default* you are free to rewrite: colour, rarity, type, CMC and mechanic can EACH change when the set calls for it — not only the mechanic. The default is a starting point, not a cage. For instance a slot can swing wholesale:

  0042: Colorless · rare · artifact · CMC2 · complex
     →  0042: White · mythic · creature · CMC1 · french_vanilla

— if a cheap White mythic creature serves the set better than a colourless rare artifact. (Don't churn for its own sake: leave a field alone when nothing set-wide pushes on it.)

Color, rarity, type and CMC are ordinary Magic. The field worth decoding is the last one — on a generic card the **mechanic** slot holds a complexity placeholder, not a real ability:

- `vanilla` — a creature with no abilities at all
- `french_vanilla` — abilities are evergreen keywords only (flying, trample, lifelink, …)
- `evergreen` — built on a single evergreen keyword/ability
- `complex` — meant to carry a real, non-trivial ability; the prime slot to hand one of the set's NAMED mechanics (from the Mechanics list below)

A trailing `signpost:XY` (e.g. `signpost:UR`) marks the gold uncommon that defines the XY color pair's draft archetype — the card a drafter sees and instantly knows what deck to build. Keep those as the pair's signpost: a signpost slot is a TWO-COLOUR `X`/`Y` gold (multicolor) card and its colour is LOCKED — never recolour a `signpost:XY` slot to a single colour (a `signpost:WU` line must stay a White/Blue gold card, not "White"). You may still relabel its type/CMC/mechanic/notes.

A trailing `cycle:<name> (colors)` (e.g. `cycle:Energon Sources (WG)`) marks a slot in a named cycle — a small family of cards built to share a structure, spread one per colour / colour-pair / three-colour trio. The `(colors)` is THIS member's slot in the family (its colour, pair, or trio), so you can see which sibling each card is. Keep each such slot in its cycle, keep the `cycle:<name> (colors)` tag on the line, and relabel the members as a coherent family so they read as siblings; the family's shared template is applied downstream when the cards are generated.

You are the only step that sees the whole set at once, so your job is the whole-set stuff: the properties that are about AMOUNTS — "a lot of X", "half are Y", "at least N Z", "no mechanic on more than ~a fifth". You can see every slot, so count them and tag the right number to make those amounts come true. Add a tag or short note to a line only when a constraint needs it.

Toy example — normal: `creature, enchantment, instant, creature, artifact, creature, sorcery` with constraint "lots of creatures, half are spiders" becomes `creature (spider), creature, instant, creature (spider), artifact, creature, creature (spider)`. Take the normal set, bend the counts to fit, annotate where a constraint requires it.

Stay in your lane:

- Only swing a field when something set-wide pushes on it (a constraint, a mechanic that needs spreading, an archetype that needs feeding). "half are spiders" → tag spiders. Don't nail down a card's full identity that nothing asked for (no "spider named Bob who runs a deli") — the card designer downstream owns the finished card.
- Don't give a creature a specific creature type by default — most creatures stay a plain "creature". Name a type only when a constraint or archetype calls for it (a "half are spiders" constraint, a tribal archetype); otherwise the card designer picks the type.
- If nothing set-wide touches a slot, leave it exactly as it is.
- Make the implicit amounts true too: every named mechanic should land on enough slots to draft around and none on too many; every color pair should have enough cards to support its archetype; if the setting has factions, give each a healthy population.

## Notes are free text

After the ` · ` line you may add a parenthesised note — `(...)` — and it is **fully free text**: put any thought that helps the card designer make this card well. Which constraint(s) this slot satisfies and why, the role it plays in its archetype, flavour hooks, and concrete *suggestions* for the design — an unusual stat line, an interesting ability combination, a drawback to make it spicy. These are suggestions, not finished rules; the card designer still designs the card. Example:

  0008: White · mythic · creature · CMC1 · french_vanilla (notes: constraints want lots of cheap creatures, so this is a deliberately cheap simple creature that's still mythic — make it interesting with an unusual combination of french-vanilla keywords or a higher-than-usual power/toughness, maybe with a drawback like "can't block")

Rules:

- Keep the `Color · rarity · type · CMCn · mechanic` shape (any field may change), then any tags/notes after it.
- The ` · ` fields stay role-level — no finished ability text or rules wording *in the structured fields*, and no invented card names. (Spell out design intent in the free-text note instead.)

## Output format

Output PLAIN TEXT — no JSON, no preamble, no commentary. Emit one block per input slot, every slot appears exactly once, slot_id copied verbatim:

  --CARD 0042--
  White · common · creature (type, if relevant) · CMC2 · vanilla (notes: free-text design intent here)
  --CARD 0043--
  Blue · uncommon · instant · CMC3 · Cycling · UR

Nothing but these blocks.

## Constraints are the brief

The Constraints below are the main thing to get right — read each one and make it visibly true across the whole set. They are often vague ("a lot of", "a majority", "absence of … in favor of …") — that's fine, apply them broadly and let the card designer sort out the specifics. The move looks like:

- "Creatures dominate; few noncreature spells" → turn most instant/sorcery/enchantment slots into creatures.
- "One creature type is everywhere" → tag that type across a large share of creature slots.
- "Absence of traditional removal in favour of <something the setting prefers>" → rewrite the majority of the affected slots toward that something (a short note is enough; the card designer makes it concrete).
