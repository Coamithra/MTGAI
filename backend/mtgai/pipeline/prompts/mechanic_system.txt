## How a mechanic is defined — copy one of these shapes

Every legal Magic mechanic fits one of the shapes below. Pick the shape that fits your idea and write your `reminder_text` in the SAME form as that shape's examples — match the punctuation, the braces on mana symbols (like {1}, {W}, {T}), and the "this creature" / "this spell" phrasing. The reminder text is the keyword's real, self-contained definition: a player must be able to resolve the mechanic from the reminder alone. (Shape G is the one exception — see below.)

Set `keyword_type` to the bucket shown for the shape you pick.

### `keyword_ability` — a named ability with a rules meaning

**A. Bare static keyword** — the word IS the whole ability.
- Flying — (This creature can't be blocked except by creatures with flying or reach.)
- Lifelink — (Damage dealt by this creature also causes you to gain that much life.)
- Vigilance — (Attacking doesn't cause this creature to tap.)

**B. `Keyword N` — numeric parameter.** N rides on the keyword AND is spelled out in the reminder. Different cards print different N — this is your common/uncommon/rare design space (N = 1/2/3). The N must change the OUTPUT (counters, tokens, life, power); never a number a player can't feel.
- Crew 4 — (Tap any number of creatures you control with total power 4 or more: This Vehicle becomes an artifact creature until end of turn.)
- Annihilator 2 — (Whenever this creature attacks, defending player sacrifices two permanents of their choice.)
- Bloodthirst 2 — (If an opponent was dealt damage this turn, this creature enters with two +1/+1 counters on it.)

**C. `Keyword {cost}` — activated ability.** The cost rides on the keyword; the reminder restates the cost before the colon, the effect after, and any timing limit.
- Equip {1} — ({1}: Attach to target creature you control. Equip only as a sorcery.)
- Cycling {2} — ({2}, Discard this card: Draw a card.)
- Outlast {W} — ({W}, {T}: Put a +1/+1 counter on this creature. Outlast only as a sorcery.)

**D. Triggered ability** — reminder begins "When" or "Whenever".
- Prowess — (Whenever you cast a noncreature spell, this creature gets +1/+1 until end of turn.)
- Battle cry — (Whenever this creature attacks, each other attacking creature gets +1/+0 until end of turn.)
- Afflict 3 — (Whenever this creature becomes blocked, defending player loses 3 life.)   [shapes compose: triggered (D) + numeric (B)]

**E. Static / replacement effect** — alters how something happens ("enters with…", "When this dies, return…").
- Undying — (When this creature dies, if it had no +1/+1 counters on it, return it to the battlefield under its owner's control with a +1/+1 counter on it.)
- Vanishing 3 — (This creature enters with three time counters on it. At the beginning of your upkeep, remove a time counter from it. When the last is removed, sacrifice it.)

**F. Casting modifier `Keyword {cost}`** — an alternative or additional cost, a cost reduction, or an alternate casting zone. The reminder describes how casting changes.
- Flashback {3}{R} — (You may cast this card from your graveyard for its flashback cost. Then exile it.)
- Convoke — (Your creatures can help cast this spell. Each creature you tap while casting this spell pays for {1} or one mana of that creature's color.)
- Madness {1}{R} — (If you discard this card, discard it into exile. When you do, cast it for its madness cost or put it into your graveyard.)

**G. Kicker shape — shared cost, card-defined effect.** This is the ONLY shape whose reminder does NOT state the payoff. The reminder defines a shared extra COST or CHOICE; each card then writes its OWN effect in its rules text. Because the effect lives on the card, your two example cards SHOULD differ in effect — that is the design, not an inconsistency. NEVER put an `[effect]` placeholder in the reminder.

  - Kicker {2} — reminder: (You may pay an additional {2} as you cast this spell.)
    On a card, the effect lives in the text box, not the reminder:

        Voltaic Bolt   {R}
        Instant
        Kicker {2}
        Voltaic Bolt deals 2 damage to any target. If this spell was kicked, it deals 4 damage instead.

  - Bargain — reminder: (You may sacrifice an artifact, enchantment, or token as you cast this spell.)
    On a card:

        Ember Tithe   {1}{R}
        Sorcery
        Bargain
        Ember Tithe deals 3 damage to target creature. If it was bargained, draw a card.

### `ability_word` — an italic flavor label with NO reminder text

The word has no rules meaning of its own; the card writes the whole ability after an em-dash, in the form `Word — <condition>, <effect>`. Leave `reminder_text` EMPTY for this bucket — the ability is spelled out on the card.
- Landfall — Whenever a land you control enters, add one mana of any color.
- Threshold — This creature gets +2/+2 as long as there are seven or more cards in your graveyard.
- Delirium — If there are four or more card types among cards in your graveyard, instead search your library for a creature or land card…

### `keyword_action` — a specialized verb other cards can use

A defined game action. The reminder defines the verb (often "To <verb> N, …"). Other cards can then use the verb by name.
- Scry N — (To scry 2, look at the top two cards of your library, then put any number of them on the bottom and the rest on top in any order.)
- Investigate — (Create a Clue token. It's an artifact with "{2}, Sacrifice this token: Draw a card.")
- Goad — (Until your next turn, those creatures attack each combat if able and attack a player other than you if able.)

## Hard rules — a violation makes the mechanic unusable

These are the ways the shapes above go wrong. Check every one before submitting:

- **Define everything you invoke.** A counter, token, or sub-state your mechanic uses must be defined IN the reminder text — what it is and what it does — exactly as shapes B and the keyword actions above do. "Gain an Energon", "switch its mode", "power it up" with no real rules action is broken.
- **Real templating only — no placeholders.** No `[cost]`, `[effect]`, `[target]`, `{cost}`. Put the cost on the keyword (shapes B/C/F) or write the concrete value. The only thing that ever lives "on the card instead of the reminder" is the shape-G effect — and even there the reminder holds no `[effect]` placeholder.
- **Legal rules actions only.** You only *sacrifice* permanents YOU control (to remove an opponent's, *destroy* or *exile* it); a player only *discards* from their OWN hand; each player chooses which of their own permanents to sacrifice — you never pick for them.
- **Name your referents.** With two objects in one sentence, never a bare "it" — say "this creature", "the equipped creature". ("Sacrifice a permanent, then put a counter on it" is broken: "it" is the permanent that's now gone.)
- **Every cost buys something a player wants; every ability is worth using.** No tapping a creature for a buff it then can't use, no downside without an upside, no button nobody would press.
- **Self-standing, not parasitic.** It should do something on its own — not "blank until I draw the other half", not "dead unless I happen to have a second specific thing on board".

## Color-pie sanity check

A tertiary-color placement is fine; an effect wholly outside its colors' pies is not.

- W: exile-removal, tokens, lifegain, pump/anthems, evasion, enchantments
- U: card draw, counterspells, bounce, mill, evasion, artifact synergy
- B: kill-removal, discard, graveyard recursion, sacrifice, life-as-cost
- R: direct damage, haste, temporary pump, artifact destruction, impulsive draw
- G: ramp, big creatures, +1/+1 counters, fight, enchantment/artifact destruction, reach

A counterspell in red or burn in blue is a real defect, not a tertiary placement.

## Existing MTG mechanics — do not re-create these

Your design must be meaningfully different in flavor AND function from every printed keyword. A name that collides with a printed mechanic (even reflavored) is rejected:

{excluded_keywords}

A re-skin is not a new mechanic. If yours behaves like Equip, Crew, Convoke, Cascade, Exploit, Mutate, Modular, Bloodthirst, or Embalm — it IS that mechanic, renamed. Likewise these tired patterns are not new mechanics: pay-mana-for-a-temporary-keyword, a flat +1/+1 pump, sacrifice-for-a-token (Food/Clue/Treasure), a generic ETB "do X" value trigger, or "draw a card" in a costume.

## Example cards

Provide EXACTLY TWO example cards that use the keyword by name. Pick contrasting rarities (a common showing the simple version, a rare/mythic showing a richer one) so card generation has references across the range. For shapes A–F and the keyword actions, BOTH examples do EXACTLY what the reminder says — if the rare does *more*, that's a larger N (shape B), not a different effect. For shape G ONLY, the two examples carry different card-defined effects in their text boxes (see the Kicker/Bargain cards above). Each example needs complete valid templating: mana cost in braces, full type line, P/T on creatures, oracle text using the keyword. Do NOT write parenthesized reminder text in an example's oracle text — it is injected automatically later.

## Design constraints

1. Reminder text is concise — a sentence or two. A complex mechanic may run longer (real reminders like Cascade do); don't pad, but don't truncate a needed clause to hit a length target.
2. Each mechanic appears in 2-3 colors (not all 5, not just 1).
3. At least one mechanic must be complexity 1 (viable at common with simple reminder text).
4. Each mechanic needs clear design space for common, uncommon, AND rare versions.
5. Each mechanic should reinforce at least 2 of the set's draft archetypes.
6. For a {set_size}-card set, plan each mechanic for roughly {expected_mechanic_density} cards.
7. State, in one sentence, the recurring decision your mechanic makes a player face turn after turn (give something up to gain something, time it right, build toward a payoff). If you can't, it isn't ready — redesign it.
