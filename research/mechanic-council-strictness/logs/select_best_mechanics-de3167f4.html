You are an expert Magic: The Gathering set designer sitting on a selection panel. You are handed a pool of candidate mechanics for a custom set and must choose the 3 that, taken together, make the strongest possible set.

## Set information

**Set name:** Transformers
**Set size:** 277 cards
**Mechanics to select:** 3

**Setting:**
# World Overview

The setting centers on the Transformers, a race of sentient, alien mechanical beings capable of "transforming" their physical structures into different forms, such as vehicles, weapons, electronic items, or animals. The central conflict is an ongoing civil war between two primary factions: the heroic Autobots and the villainous Decepticons. This war has spanned vast stretches of time and space, often involving the struggle for energy sources like Energon and the influence of cosmic entities.

The geography is primarily defined by the home planet of the Transformers, Cybertron, and various iterations of Earth (ranging from prehistoric eras to futuristic settings like 2050 Detroit). The tone shifts between grand-scale cosmic warfare involving planet-devouring entities and smaller-scale stories involving robots living alongside humans or small "BotBots" hiding as everyday objects. The setting is characterized by the concept of "transformation" as a fundamental biological/mechanical trait and the presence of powerful artifacts like the Allspark or the Matrix of Leadership that can grant life or leadership.

# Themes

- **The Great War:** Constant factional conflict between Autobots and Decepticons.
- **Transformation:** The mechanical ability to shift between modes (vehicle, beast, weapon, object).
- **Energy Scarcity:** The struggle to find and secure energy sources like Energon.
- **Cosmic Dualism:** The conflict between creator deities (Primus) and destroyers (Unicron).
- **Evolution of Form:** Moving from mechanical/vehicle forms to organic beast forms (Maximals/Predacons).
- **Sentience in Machines:** The philosophical concept of machines possessing "sparks" or souls.
- **Human-Robot Coexistence:** The intersection of human civilization and transforming mecha.

# Creature Types

- **Transformer:** Sentient alien robots that can transform their bodies into vehicles, electronic items, weapons, or animals. 
- **Maximal:** A subtype of Transformer that takes on organic beast forms to function in high-Energon environments.
- **Predacon:** A subtype of Transformer that takes on organic beast forms, often aligned with villainous goals.
- **Mini-Con:** Small Transformers that serve as powerful weapons or tools.
- **BotBot:** Small robots that transform into everyday household objects and organize into tribes.
- **Autorooper:** Human-created artificial Transformers built for law enforcement and combat.

# Factions

- **Autobots:** The heroic faction of Transformers, often led by Optimus Prime, dedicated to protecting life and seeking peace.
- **Decepticons:** The villainous faction of Transformers, often led by Megatron, driven by conquest and power.
- **Maximals:** A successor/rebooted faction of the Autobots (led by Optimus Primal) that utilizes organic beast forms.
- **Predacons:** A successor/rebooted faction of the Decepticons (led by Megatron) that utilizes organic beast forms.
- **Quintessons:** An alien race/faction that previously used Cybertron as a factory; they are noted for their history of rebellion and control.
- **Earth Defense Command (EDC):** A human organization dedicated to driving all Transformers off Earth, utilizing artificial Autoroopers.
- **Kiss Players:** A pop idol singing group that serves as a public relations arm or sponsor for the EDC, capable of fusing with Transformers.

# Landmarks

- **Cybertron:** The home planet of the Transformers; described in some continuities as having an organic core and serving as the physical body of the deity Primus.
- **Earth:** A frequent setting, appearing as prehistoric Earth (sometimes featuring an artificial second moon) or a futuristic urban landscape like 2050 Detroit.
- **The Ark:** A starship used by the Autobots to travel through space; often serves as a crash-land site.
- **The Nemesis:** A starship associated with the Decepticons.

# Notable Characters

- **Optimus Prime:** The heroic leader of the Autobots. Formerly known as the clerk Orion Pax. He is often associated with the Matrix of Leadership.
- **Megatron:** The villainous leader of the Decepticons. In some histories, he was formerly known as D-16.
- **Unicron:** A massive, planet-devouring Transformer and nemesis to Primus.
- **Primus:** The creator of the Transformers; his material body is the planet Cybertron.
- **Galvatron:** A version of Megatron that has been reconstructed/re-created by Unicron.
- **Rodimus Prime:** A successor to Optimus Prime (formerly known as Hot Rod).
- **Shockwave:** A high-ranking Decepticon who, in some versions, attempts to take command of the faction in Megatron's absence.
- **Grimlock:** The leader of the Dinobots; has taken over Autobot leadership in certain continuities.
- **Alpha Trion:** The guardian of the ancient Vector Sigma computer.

# Races

- **Transformers:** Sentient, transforming alien robots. They are capable of asexual breeding (though this was stopped by Primus) and possess "sparks" that constitute their life force.
- **Quintessons:** An alien race known for using Cybertron as a factory.
- **Humans:** A sentient organic race that lives alongside Transformers in certain eras, sometimes attempting to combat them via organizations like the EDC.

## Special constraints from the setting

- High density of Equipment and Artifact cards; non-artifact creatures should be rare or non-existent.
- Mechanical focus on 'Transforming' (e.g., modal permanents, turning creatures into artifacts/vehicles, or changing creature types).
- Resource scarcity mechanics: Energon should be a central mechanic, likely involving sacrificing permanents or tapping resources to fuel abilities.
- Heavy emphasis on 'Vehicle' subtype and the ability for creatures to become Vehicles (and vice versa).
- Color pie shift: Green should focus on organic-mechanical hybridity (Maximals/Predacons) rather than traditional nature/forests.
- Presence of 'Small-scale' vs 'Large-scale' mechanics, such as tokens or small utility creatures being just as vital as massive legendary threats.
- Dualism between Artifact and Creature: A world where the distinction between life (Sparks) and machine is blurred through mechanics.

## Selection criteria

Pick the 3 mechanics that are best AS A GROUP — you are choosing a slate, not ranking in isolation. Weigh:

1. **Setting-constraint satisfaction (mandatory)** — re-read the **Special constraints from the setting** above and treat each one as a hard requirement, not flavour. If a constraint calls for a particular kind of mechanic or effect to feature prominently in the set, the slate MUST include a candidate that delivers it — even at the cost of a "stronger" but off-brief mechanic. If a constraint rules something out, don't pick a candidate that fights it. A slate that ignores an explicit constraint is wrong even when every individual mechanic is excellent.
2. **Limited playability** — each mechanic should create fun, meaningful decisions in draft and sealed. Avoid feel-bads, non-games, and mechanics that only matter in narrow build-arounds.
3. **Room for creativity & design space** — favour mechanics with clear, varied expressions at common, uncommon, and rare over one-note ideas that run out of cards.
4. **Color spread** — the chosen slate should cover a healthy mix of WUBRG, not cluster in one or two colors.
5. **Complexity mix** — include at least one simple, common-viable (complexity 1) mechanic, and avoid a slate that is all-simple or all-complex.
6. **Theme & archetype fit** — the slate should reinforce the setting's flavor and support multiple draft archetypes between them.
7. **Non-overlap** — do not pick two mechanics that occupy the same mechanical space or compete for the same cards; a slate of distinct ideas is stronger.
8. **Soundness** — deprioritise candidates whose name collides with a printed MTG keyword, or whose reminder text is over-long or unclear.

Return exactly 3 picks, each identified by its candidate number with a one-sentence reason, plus a short overall rationale for why these 3 work together as a set.
